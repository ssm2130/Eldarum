Aetherius, Crusader of Wrath
Weapon (Longblow), Artifact (Relic of the Scourge)
Requires attunement by a a character who is proficient with Long Bows
Given the power within the Relics of the Scourge, you are only able to attune to one of these artifacts.  Should you attempt to attune to another Relic, you are unable until you drop attunement to the relic you are already attuned to.
While attuned to this item, you are granted a +1 bonus to your Attack and Damage rolls when you use this bow.  On a hit, the weapon deals an additional 1d6 bludgeoning damage.
 
One with the Earth:
1/Long Rest:
The wielder may summon the power of the earth to drag their enemies to the ground. Choose one creature you can see within 300 feet.  Yellow strips of magical energy loop around the creature.  The target must succeed on a Strength saving throw vs your Spell DC or its flying speed (if any) is reduced to 0 feet for 1 minute.  An airborne creature affected by this spell safely descends at 60 feet per round until it reaches the ground.
 
Trick shot:
3 charges (refresh short or long rest).  All charges refresh on a short rest.
When you attune to Aetherius, choose 2 Arcane shots that you have access to from the Arcane Archer - Arcane Shot Options section from the list below.
Once per turn when you fire an arrow from Aetherius as part of the Attack action, you can expend a charge to apply one of your Arcane Shot options to that arrow.  You decide to use the option when the arrow hits a creature, unless the option doesn't involve an attack roll.
Arcane Shot Options:
If an option requires a saving throw, your Arcane Shot save DC equals your spellcasting DC.
Banishing Arrow:
You use abjuration magic to try to temporarily banish your target to a harmless location in the Feywild. The creature hit by the arrow must also succeed on a Charisma saving throw or be banished. While banished in this way, the target’s speed is 0, and it is incapacitated. At the end of its next turn, the target reappears in the space it vacated or in the nearest unoccupied space if that space is occupied.
Beguiling Arrow:
Your enchantment magic causes this arrow to temporarily beguile its target. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 psychic damage, and choose one of your allies within 30 feet of the target. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw, or it is charmed by the chosen ally until the start of your next turn. This effect ends early if the chosen ally attacks the charmed target, deals damage to it, or forces it to make a saving throw.
Bursting Arrow:
You imbue your arrow with force energy drawn from the school of evocation. The energy detonates after your attack. Immediately after the arrow hits the creature, the target and all other creatures within 10 feet of it take 2d6 force damage each.
Enfeebling Arrow:
You weave necromantic magic into your arrow. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 necrotic damage. The target must also succeed on a Constitution saving throw, or the damage dealt by its weapon attacks is halved until the start of your next turn.
Grasping Arrow:
When this arrow strikes its target, conjuration magic creates grasping, poisonous brambles, which wrap around the target. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 poison damage, its speed is reduced by 10 feet, and it takes 2d6 slashing damage the first time on each turn it moves 1 foot or more without teleporting. The target or any creature that can reach it can use its action to remove the brambles with a successful Strength (Athletics) check against your Arcane Shot save DC. Otherwise, the brambles last for 1 minute or until you use this option again.
Piercing Arrow:
You use transmutation magic to give your arrow an ethereal quality. When you use this option, you don’t make an attack roll for the attack. Instead, the arrow shoots forward in a line, which is 1 foot wide and 30 feet long, before disappearing. The arrow passes harmlessly through objects, ignoring cover. Each creature in that line must make a Dexterity saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes damage as if it were hit by the arrow, plus an extra 1d6 piercing damage. On a successful save, a target takes half as much damage.
Seeking Arrow:
Using divination magic, you grant your arrow the ability to seek out a target. When you use this option, you don’t make an attack roll for the attack. Instead, choose one creature you have seen in the past minute. The arrow flies toward that creature, moving around corners if necessary and ignoring three-quarters cover and half cover. If the target is within the weapon’s range and there is a path large enough for the arrow to travel to the target, the target must make a Dexterity saving throw. Otherwise, the arrow disappears after traveling as far as it can. On a failed save, the target takes damage as if it were hit by the arrow, plus an extra 1d6 force damage, and you learn the target’s current location. On a successful save, the target takes half as much damage, and you don’t learn its location.
Shadow Arrow:
You weave illusion magic into your arrow, causing it to occlude your foe’s vision with shadows. The creature hit by the arrow takes an extra 2d6 psychic damage, and it must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be unable to see anything farther than 5 feet away until the start of your next turn.
Ancient spell book
Spell Book:
This is an ancient spell book containing spells from a forgotten magic.  Many of the pages are destroyed by time and are illegible.  A few of the pages are in tact and contain 5 complete spells.
After learning at least 1 Abrakary spell, your detect magic spell casting can now identify this magic type.
Spells:
Chicanerous Semblance
1st Level Abrakary
Casting Time: 1 bonus action
Range: 30 feet
Components: V, S
Duration: 1 minute
 
   As an action, you create a perfect illusion of yourself that lasts for 1 minute, or until you lose your concentration (as if you were concentrating on a spell). The illusion appears in an unoccupied space that you can see within 30 feet of you. As a bonus action on your turn, you can move the illusion up to 30 feet to a space you can see, but it must remain within 120 feet of you.
   For the duration, you can cast spells as though you were in the illusion’s space, but you must use your own senses. Additionally, when both you and your illusion are within 5 feet of a creature that can see the illusion, you have advantage on attack rolls against that creature, given how distracting the illusion is to the target.
 
Pensive Dissolution (Ritual)
1st Level Abrakary
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: Touch
Components: V, S, M (A vial containing pure water)
Duration: 1 minute (memory strand until dispelled)
 
   As part of casting this spell, you shed a single tear. The tear contains a single memory up to 5 minutes in duration and transforms it into a tangible string of glowing energy which can be placed in a vial filled with pure water.  The strand persists until dispelled.  You can attempt to cast this spell on a creature who must make a Wisdom saving throw to resist shedding the memory tear.  The creature must be alive or have died within the last 1 minute.  On a failed save the creature sheds a surface memory in the tear.
  The memory tear appears as a tiny, weightless, slowly moving white strand while in liquid.  As an action, you can cast this spell again while pouring the vial into a container of pure water.  Anyone dipping their face into the water experiences the memory as if they were an observer.  The memory may then be gathered and put back into the vial.
If you are concentrating on a spell such as detect thoughts when you cast this spell on another creature, you can extract a specific memory from that creature if they failed their save against detect thoughts.  Additionally, a creature who is under the extraction of the deeper level of detect thoughts automatically fails its save against this spell.
  At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell at 4th level as an action rather than a ritual, you simultaneously cast the detect thoughts spell with this spell.  The target must make a Wisdom save or fail the deep probe portion of that spell and shed a memory tear of the memory you designate.
Replenishment (Ritual)
2nd Level Abrakary
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: Touch
Components: V, S, M (any container up to 1 cubic foot filled with food* or drink*)
Duration: 8 hours
 
   You touch a container that contains food or drink.  The food or drink placed in the container is consumed as part of the spell casting, but reappears in the container instantaneously.  For the duration, an unlimited amount of food or drink can be consumed from the container.  It produces that same quantity of food or drink that was placed in the container every 6 seconds.  When the spell ends, any food or drink that has not been consumed magically disappears and the container is a clean and empty.
 
  At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell using a 3rd level slot, the duration increases to 24 hours.  When you cast this spell using a 5th level slot, the duration increases to 1 week.
(* a quantity of food or drink that is consumed by the casting of the spell)
Steadfast Object
3rd Level Abrakary
Casting Time: 1 minute
Range: Touch
Components: V, M (any object up to 10 cubic feet)
Duration: 1 hour
   You touch an object no larger than 10 cubic feet, which causes it to become magically fixed in place.  Until the duration ends, it is dispelled, or you end the spell as an action, the object doesn’t move, even if it is defying gravity.  The object can hold up to 8,000 pounds of weight.  More weight causes the object to deactivate and fall.  A creature can use an action to make a DC 30 Strength check, moving the fixed object up to 10 feet on a success.
 
  At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell at 4th level, the duration extends to 8 hours.  At 5th level, the duration extends to 1 day.  At 6th level, the duration extends to 10 days.  At 7th level the duration extends to 1 year.  At 8th level the duration extends to "until dispelled".
 
Planar Void
4th Level Abrakary
Casting Time: 1 action
Range: 150 feet
Components: V, S, M (a pinch of meteorite dust)
Duration: Instantaneous
 
   A streak of energy devoid of all light shoots forth from your finger to a point you choose within range and it instantly expands into a temporary rift to the void.  Each creature in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on that point must make a Dexterity saving throw.  A target takes 8d6 force damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.
   The void spreads encompass all areas within the sphere, including around corners. Creatures who fail their save have their speed become 0 until the start of the caster’s next turn.
   At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 5th level or higher, the damage increases by 1d6 for each slot level above 4th level.
GM Notes
Identify Cast or with DC 18 Arcana check:
This spell book is from an ancient magic called Abrakary.
A Wizard may scribe a spell in their spell book and attempt to cast the spell.  The magic of Abrakary is unstable and has a 5% chance of having unexpected results.  Roll 1d20 when you cast a spell.  On a 1, roll 1d100 and consult the Erratic Magic table.  The rolled result takes effect along with the spells effects.
Counter spell can be used against this type of magic, but suffers a -2 penalty to do so.  There is no auto dispell, it must be a roll.
History:
Abrakary magic was the primary magic prior to the Magi-bane imposed by the gods.  After the Magi-bane, Nila (goddess of nature) removed all knowledge of the magic from the world.  It took 689 years for magic to return to the world.  When it returned, it was in the form of the schools of magic that we are familiar with today.
Abrakary was forever impacted by the Magi-bane imposed by Nila.  Although it is possible to cast Abrakary spells, they have a level of instability that will remain unless Nila chooses to resolve this instability.  Any attempt to cast Abrakary spells has a 5% chance of an unexpected result occurring.
Apparatus of Mastery
Wondrous Item, very rare (requires attunement)
Made by a master Artificer, this pair of gloves has clockwork gears buried deep inside.  The shapes of several tools can be made out within the back of the glove.  Amber and purple stones faintly glow inset into the right glove.  The Glove of Mastery is a boon for anyone seeking to expand their ability to craft items.
While attuned to the item, you can press the purple gem and trace the pattern of an elaborate door on any flat surface.  Once completed, 1 minute later the pattern makes a doorway to an extradimensional workshop that measures 50 ft in each direction.  The workshop is purpose built for crafting and contains all of the required tools for Alchemy, brewing, calligraphy, carpentry, map making, cobbling, food preparation, glassblowing, jewelry making, leatherwork, masonry, painting, potting, smithing, tinkering, weaving, and woodcarving.  Purpose built areas within the space include a forge, glassblowers furnace, kiln, and cooktops witch require no coal or wood to burn.  Instead, an adjustable lever determines the heat produced by each of these sources.  The workshop also has 10 cubic feet of cold storage.
A small library resides within the workshop detailing various techniques for utilizing the tools to craft various items.  In addition, the glove itself has a number of tools built into it. By pressing on small inset buttons, a tool springs forth out of the glove for immediate use.  These tools are hand tools and items used in tinkering, leatherworking, shoe making, woodcarving, weaving, potters tools, and jewelers tools.
The glove has 2 charges.  While attuned, the user can depress the amber button an speak a discipline.  The glove grants the user proficiency in a tool of their choice for 24 hours.  You can have 2 such proficiencies at a time.  The glove refreshes all of its charges daily at dawn.  When enacted, the glove assists you in your work as it is imbued with the knowledge of its creator.
Notes: Proficiency: Alchemist's Supplies, Proficiency: Brewer's Supplies, Proficiency: Calligrapher's Supplies, Proficiency: Carpenter's Tools, Proficiency: Cartographer's Tools, Proficiency: Cobbler's Tools, Proficiency: Cook's Utensils, Proficiency: Glassblower's Tools, Proficiency: Jeweler's Tools, Proficiency: Leatherworker's Tools, Proficiency: Mason's Tools, Proficiency: Painter's Supplies, Proficiency: Potter's Tools, Proficiency: Smith's Tools, Proficiency: Tinker's Tools, Proficiency: Weaver's Tools, Proficiency: Woodcarver's Tools, Requires attunement, Artificer

GM Notes
Rak Bal'uroth, The Soul Crusher
Rak Bal'uroth is constructed of Infernal Iron with a large Titan shard hammer head and a smaller shard in the pommel.  The titan shards are a remnants of great titan Rak Tulkhesh, commanded by Balthazar, The Lord of Fallen Souls.  This formidable weapon grants the wielder great power, but its evil force taints the soul of those who kill with it.  When a creature is killed by this weapon and soul consumed, a black smoke emanates from the Warhammer.
Dormant:

Edible Book of Recipes
Wondrous item, very rare
This tome of cooking recipes is enchanted to turn sheets of fine paper into delectable meals. You can write a simple recipe on 1 of the book’s pages over the course of 1 minute. As you do, the paper becomes aromatic, smelling like the cuisine you’re writing. You can eat the sheet of paper as an action and find that it has the taste, texture, and smell of the food described on its page. The sheet of paper takes the place of a meal.
If you add a more complex recipe to the book that takes at least 10 minutes to write—potentially using sensory language, descriptions of fine ingredients, or illustrations of the dish—eating the delicious sheet of paper provides enough nourishment to sustain a creature for 1 day. A creature that eats from the book using one of these complex recipes gains and has their hit point maximum increase by 1d10 + their proficiency bonus until they finish a long rest. In addition, if the creature is diseased or poisoned, it can reattempt the saving throw to end it. A creature can only gain the benefits of a complex recipe once every 24 hours.
In addition, you can use this book as a spellcasting focus when casting the create food and water spell, should you know it. When you do, the food and drink you create can be aromatic and flavorful: recreating recipes of your choice from the book.
The book can hold up to 50 pages. The book has 22 blank pages as well as 10 filled pages containing complex recipes.
When all the pages are removed from the book or you attempt to rebind it early, roll a d20. On a 1, the tome bursts into flame and leaves the air in a 60-foot radius smelling like wine and fine cooking. The smell remains for 30 days. After 1 minute, the book’s tattered remains transform into a bountiful meal, conjuring a heroes’ feast as if cast by the spell. The book is then destroyed. On any roll other than a 1, the book can be rebound using fine paper costing no less than 5 gp per page.

Landscape Features:
Greenveil Forest: On the entire Western edge of the Quag.  Home to the Elven Realms.

Settlements:
Beggar’s Hole: A hidden village masked by Druid magical Illusion.  The roads leading into the Village appear to be thick impassable swamp covered in Brambles.  The Druids utilize the natural Snag and Sparge Brambles to the North and South of the Village to mask the village with a continuation of these brambles.  This is many of the undesirable races live as they are disliked or not allowed in the Kingdom.  Unbeknownst to the Kingdom, they export various ceramics.  The Shepherds smuggle goods in and out of the Village.  That is the job of Finnius Flax in Toufort.
Camp Elliswich: Ran by The Shepherds secretly.  This camps’ primary function is to operate as a front of the ceramic goods coming from Beggar’s Hole.  This camp is used to move items to the East.
Crossing Camp: Ran by The Shepherds secretly.  This camps’ primary function is to operate as a front of the ceramic goods coming from Beggar’s Hole.  This camp is used to move items to the South and West.
Narrow Camp: Ran by The Shepherds secretly.  This camps’ primary function is to operate as a front of the ceramic goods coming from Beggar’s Hole.  This camp is used to move items to the Elven Realm.
Ridge Camp: Ran by The Shepherds secretly.  This camps’ primary function is to operate as a front of the ceramic goods coming from Beggar’s Hole.  This camp is used to move items to Thrail in the North.
Other Locations:
Snag Bramble: Extremely difficult, nearly impassable part of the quag.  Consisting of tightly packed thorn covered brush.
Sparge Bramble: Extremely difficult, nearly impassable part of the quag.  Consisting of tightly packed thorn covered brush.

GM Notes
Poison Darts
Mechanical Trap
When a creature steps on a hidden pressure plate, poison-tipped darts shoot from spring-loaded or pressurized tubes cleverly embedded in the surrounding walls. An area might include multiple pressure plates, each one rigged to its own set of darts.
The tiny holes in the walls are obscured by dust and cobwebs, or cleverly hidden amid bas-reliefs, murals, or frescoes that adorn the walls. The DC to spot them is 15. With a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check, a character can deduce the presence of the pressure plate from variations in the mortar and stone used to create it, compared to the surrounding floor. Wedging an iron spike or other object under the pressure plate prevents the trap from activating. Stuffing the holes with cloth or wax prevents the darts contained within from launching.
The trap activates when more than 20 pounds of weight is placed on the pressure plate, releasing four darts. Each dart makes a ranged attack with a +8 bonus against a random target within 10 feet of the pressure plate (vision is irrelevant to this attack roll). (If there are no targets in the area, the darts don’t hit anything.) A target that is hit takes 2 (1d4) piercing damage and must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw, taking 11 (2d10) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.

Devour the Weak. Whenever The Soul Eater damages a living creature, that creature must roll a Constitution saving throw (DC 12). On a failed save, The Soul Eater drains one point from the creature's Strength score and transfers it to the wielder (increasing to a maximum of 25 Strength) until the next short rest or long rest. Creatures brought to 0 Strength by this feature are slain.
Gluttonous Evolution. If The Soul Eater devours enough strength from Devour the Weak to bring the wielder to 25 Strength, then the blade shifts form into a larger, jagged blade that seeps shadow. Until the wielder's next short rest or long rest, all attacks with this sword deal an additional 2d6 necrotic damage. After the short rest, the attuned creature must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 16) or have the now-hungry weapon, energy spent, devour the wielder's soul when they die.
Sentience. The Soul Eater is a sentient, chaotic evil weapon with an intelligence of 16, a wisdom of 12, and a charisma of 18. It has hearing and darkvision out to a range of 60 ft. This weapon can understand abyssal & common, and can communicate with anyone who's wielded it telepathically. While you are attuned to it, The Soul Eater also understands every language you know.
Personality. The Soul Eater is cold, vicious, and seeks violence at any opportunity. It wishes to be used by merciless creatures who will feed it when ordered to.

Star Metal
Star Metal
8,000 gp per pound (offered by Kul Buldar King)
Star metal is the leftover material from a shooting star. It is extremely rare and in high demand because it could be forged with common metals to make durable weapons of great strength. When found, these stones are usually small, about thumb-sized, though ogre-head-sized pieces have known to have been discovered.
Alloyed with common metals, this material increased the strength, sharpness, flexibility, and durability of weapons, making them ideal for enchantment.
Star metal is reputed to enhance the strength and duration of any enchantments placed on blades that included it, but it was difficult to prove such claims.
In addition to weapon smithing, tiny pieces are sometimes sliced and polished for inlay work

Storm League:
A secret society whose charge is to ensure the perpetual power of the storm. The storm is the bringer of cleansing, life, and change. Devout worship of the Storm Lord ensures the perpetuation of life to Eldarum.
Toros, The Storm Lord, wields the power nature to shape the world of Eldarum. He has the power to block out the sun, reshape the landscape, and bring life giving water to the plants and animals that require it to survive. Many people across the world call on Toros for calm weather on the seas. Toros is also called upon in times of war. Controlling the weather and using the power of lightning grants the wielder great power to defeat their enemies. Nila, the Goddess of Nature, calls upon Toros as the equalizer. In times of the past, massive cyclones engulfed Eldarum as the gods struggled for power over this plane. A secret organization called the Storm League ensures that Toros has the power to continue his charge.
The group is led by (The Three). The group of three are very old, very wise beings.  The organization was started by the Ents of old, two of which still reside within the world.
Information was not shared with you about the members on Chanaire, Xaraquin, or Cilobria.  You are however aware of a presence on these continents, although The Three do not reside on those continents.  Since they were not part of your quest, the details were left out.

Talon of Authority
Wonderous Item, Rarity Varies (Requires attunement by a Sorcerer or spellcaster with Meta-magic spell alteration feature)
These shards have been shaped and imbued with magical energy by a long lost Sorcerer.  The attunement to this item requires physical pain.  Unlike normal attunement, this attunement does not count against the number of attunement slots available to you.  To attune to this item, you must tear off your fingernail and replace it with the Talon.  Doing so causes 1d12 piercing damage when the nail is removed and 4d10 piercing damage when the talon is fit.  Once done, the talon is permanently attack until death and prevents that fingernail from growing.

Wondrous Item, Rare
When rolled up, this tent is the same size as a two-person tent.  It weighs 20 pounds and can be attached to the bottom of a backpack.
When set up, this tent appears to be of a normal size on the outside.  Inside, it uses extra-dimensional space to create a 1200 square foot area containing a main room, washroom, kitchen, dining room, and 6 side bedrooms.  It is fulling furnished with tables, beds, chests, wood-burning stove and lanterns.  The space is vary airy and allows the natural light through the material.  The tent can comfortably hold 12 people and can maintain a comfortable, dry environment.

Placing a lavish tent inside an extra-dimensional space created by a bag of holding, handy haversack, portable hole, or similar item instantly destroys both items and opens a gate to the Astral Plane. The gate is one-way only and can't be reopened.

The Stratagem (Relic of the Scourge)
The Stratagem
Wonderous Item (Amulet), Artifact (Relic of the Scourge)
Requires Attunement by a spellcaster
Long ago a powerful mage desired above all else to have full control over his portrayal to others. Utilizing the power of a great shard, he combined illusion magic with transmutation magic to shroud his true self behind powerful magic. It is likely that for this reason, the amulet also maintained a fairly mundane appearance that helped it be overlooked by scholars as it doesn't have the flash and jewels of some of the other relics of the scourge.  That seems to be intentional as the true power within the Stratagem is only revealed to a creature who shows it loyalty.
While attuned to this amulet, you gain the following benefits:
You gain a +1 bonus to spell attacks
You learn the Minor Illusion cantrip
You gain Proficiency in Deception.  If you already have Proficiency, you gain Expertise instead.
The Stratagem has 10 charges and it regains 1d6+4 charges daily upon a long rest. 
Spells.  You can use an action to expend 1 or more of its charges to cast one of the following spells from it, using your spell save DC and spell attack bonus:
Disguise Self (1 Charge), Silent Image (1 charge), Alter Self (2 charges), Barkskin (2 charges), Blur (2 charges), Darkvision (2 charges), Enlarge/Reduce (2 charges), Invisibility (2 charges), Magic Aura (2 charges), Mirror Image (2 charges)
GM Notes
Awakened:
You remained Attuned to The Stratagem, but an attunement slot is no longer required.
The Stratagem feels your kinship with it.  As this connection forms, you begin to merge with the amulet and it becomes difficult to distinguish you from the amulet.
As your attunement gets stronger, you gain the following benefits ( in addition to all previous benefits):
You gain a +2 bonus to spell attacks
You gain a +5 bonus to Deception
You are hidden from divination magic.  You can't be targeted by such magic or perceived through magical scrying sensors.
The Stratagem now has 20 charges and it regains 2d8+4 charges daily upon a long rest. 
Spells.  In addition to its previous spells, you now can also cast the following spells:
Blink (3 Charges), Fly (3 charges), Major Image (3 charges), Greater Invisibility (3 charges), Mislead (5 charges)
Exalted:
As your bond with The Stratagem reaches it maximum, the amulet completely merges with your form and becomes invisible to any creature, even if they have true sight.
You can now utilize the full power of the The Stratagem and gain the following benefits ( in addition to all previous benefits):
You gain a +3 bonus to spell attacks
When you make a deception check, the d20 roll, you can treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.
The Stratagem shares its ability to deceive the eyes with you.  It projects an illusion that makes you appear to be standing in a place near your actual location, causing any creature to have disadvantage on attack rolls against you.  If you take damage, the property ceases to function until the start of your next turn.  This property is suppressed while you are incapacitated, restrained, or otherwise unable to move.  Creatures that do not rely on sight, as with blindsight, or can see through illusions, as with truesight still know your exact location.  As a bonus action, you can unveil the illusion creatures of your choice within 100' of you.  You remain unveiled to them until you use your bonus action to again displace your location.
The Stratagem now has 40 charges and it regains 3d10+10 charges daily upon a long rest. 
Spells. In addition to its previous spells, you now can also cast the following spells:
Mirage Arcane (7 Charges), Project Image (7 charges), Glibness (8 charges)

Topaz Annihilator
Weapon (Firearm), Legendary (Requires Attunement)
This magic ranged weapon resembles a musket, but in lieu of any ammunition, it holds a glowing yellow scale from a topaz dragon in its heart.
The weapon has a normal range of 100 feet and a long range of 300 feet, and it has the two-handed property. It deals 2d6 necrotic damage on a hit. If this damage reduces a creature or object to 0 hit points, the target is reduced to dust. A creature reduced to dust can be restored to life only by a true resurrection or wish spell.
While the weapon is on your person, you can use an action to cast the disintegrate spell (save DC 18). Once this property is used, it can’t be used again until the next dawn.
