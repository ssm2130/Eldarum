Asceveron

Frontier peninsula where crowns fray and secrets pay in salt.

Asceveron is the theater of your campaign writ in salt-stung air: a coastal march claimed in competing maps by Stammont heirs, Zrelian admiralties, charter companies, and genius loci who remember older contracts. Ports here weigh anchors with prayer; alleys weigh loyalty with silver. Titan relics rumor beneath ridgelines; corsairs rumor beneath polite society.

Economic life

Tar pits, pearl beds, and volcanic glass drive smuggling dialects comprehensible only to Customs ghosts. Mercenary contracts circulate faster than royal edicts; notaries wear chain beneath robes. Adventurers arrive chasing patron payments, relic buyers, or cures named in prophecies whispered by itinerant clergy.

Power brokers

Harbor syndicates feud with inland ranchers over water rights during ash seasons. Patrons such as Kul Buldar finance expeditions toward Zehas or Titan ossuaries in the peaks—missions balanced between archaeology and arson.

Dangers

  • Political: Informants for Stammont, Zrelia, and third-party coin.
  • Occult: Cult cells—especially devotees of forbidden courts—seek footholds near storm-singed temples.
  • Natural: Ash fog, rogue waves, lahar scars near volcanic vents.

Routes of note

Sailors chart passages toward Kazon when guild wars close nearer harbors. Couriers depart Baycliff with coded barrels; scholars debate whether couriers outnumber barrels. Campaign chronicles tie this peninsula to royal weddings gone plague-foul, Zrelian fleets threading storm routes, hag-haunted roads toward hidden freeholds, and volcano prayers answered in Hazoret's name—story beats narrated beat-by-beat on Campaign journal whenever you need the prose behind the rumor.
