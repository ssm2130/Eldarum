Continental overview

Asceveron is one of Eldarum’s seven continents—this page is its continental atlas hub. The name also labels a famous march on the frontier; for that campaign article (patrons, Storm Court, Kul Buldar, couriers), use Asceveron — frontier detail.

The continent’s northwest—called the Northwake in barracks and broadsheets—is a shield of peninsulas flaked by cold currents, river kingdoms fat with grain, and narrows where Zrelian hulls rehearse tomorrow’s war today. Chroniclers wax formal; soldiers call the mud the same thing with different punctuation.

The continent’s emotional weather comes from three pressures: royal highways pretending order, forests pretending innocence, and seas pretending they belong to anyone. Titan strata finger through the Highfold peaks—expeditions financed by patrons such as Kul Buldar drag relic-law up like weeds.

For ocean-scale framing and where the other continents sit relative to Northwake, read World of Eldarum; for weekdays, seasons, and trade tempo fleets synchronize to, see Calendar, weather & trade.

Greywake & Mirror Reach

The Greywake Sea separates Stammont’s batteries from Zrelian admiralties; bell codes and lantern grammars differ just enough to cause tragedies hired as statistics. The Mirror Reach inner basin calms trade argosies—pilots swear the water holds silver chalk wakes only when crowns lie well.

Pirate economics stitch both waters: Greywake corsairs rebrand as privateers when treaties blink; Asceveron smugglers treat embargoes as rotating dinner menus.

Velen, Ostro & Mir

The Three Sisters rivers braid toward Collimar: Velen carries wheat and bargemen’s folksongs; Ostro carries timber and lawsuits over splash rights; Mir carries fog thick enough to smuggle prophecies. Royal engineers pave Velen’s banks; loggers curse Ostro’s mud; priests charge Mir pilgrims for “optional clarity.”

Stammont Kingdom

Stammont styles itself the Northwake bulwark—hereditary monarchy, Privy Lattice ministries, and armies that speak supply fluently. Nobility swear on relic blades; merchants swear on cheaper paper; both oaths survive until the next profitable heresy.

Royal psychology obsesses over Zrelian sails and interior orcish pressures—supply philosophy birthed elite escort companies like the Marauders, whose disasters near Towfort still finance ballads and hospital wings.

Zrelian shores

Zrelia’s coalition coasts trade in prophecy futures and admiralty pride. Lightning-readers charge consultation fees competitive with surgery; harbor magnates lease storm-saints skiffs for parades. Raids on Stammont fishing lines are rarely signed “with love,” yet rarely lack signatures entirely.

Asceveron march

Asceveron concentrates your campaign’s chaos: frontier charters, Storm Court politics, divine attention, and expeditions toward Zehas or Titan ossuaries. Patrons bank dangerous archaeology; couriers vanish mid-contract; storms answer petitions with sarcasm.

In-world motives tied here: investigating how gods exert power through local temples; securing Storm Court influence; tracing patrons, repayments (Asnaes), and voyages toward Kazon once plotted beside travelers like Sazi.

Collimar

The capital stacks ministries on river knees—cipher vaults, harbor pilots who negotiate futures, and hospitals where curse-breakers debate physicians. Veterans rotate through on stretchers of conflicting dignity; archives smell like vinegar and anxiety.

Minwall & Greiser road

Minwall perches on the northern Velen marsh-shore—fish-smoke, rope braiding, eyes painted on prows to “confuse jealous waves.” Many soldiers’ sagas begin here; Captain Julian Welles’s childhood anchors to this salt.

The road toward Greiser ferries pilgrims and grain alike—market fairs where abbots bless barley futures beside traveling players.

Sanfeld

Orchard hills south of the capital remember softer stories—farmhouses bought with officer’s pay, cider pressed in seasons between deployments. Broadsheets rarely dignify such towns; veterans mythologize them instead.

Darkshore

Layered batteries and signal towers rehearse naval war against Zrelia—chains rise at dusk, chaplains bless cannon with irony earnest enough to stain. Civilians memorize evacuation drills; fishers resent ballast dumped by royal hulls.

Towfort & Timberwild

Towfort anchors highways threatened by Timberwild raids—granaries taste of dust and nerves. Black Tusk banners drum at ice-thaw; ranger marks and orcish drum codes argue over the same ridgelines.

Black Tusk campaigns

The Black Tusk coalition paints tusks with ash and berry dye. Chieftain Nok’tar, shaman Lag’ra, and captain Khartag choreograph ambushes blending ferocity with spiritual insult—curses finger-spoken in Orcish leave veterans shaking seasons later.

Baycliff listeners

Terraced cliffs, signal lamps, whiskey honesty—Baycliff trades jurisdiction like tide pools. Intelligence contacts name listeners such as Ian McCreaty among couriers rotating through Asceveron postings.

Kazon & island routes

Kazon sits westward on Lucani trade arcs—free port where laws rent by the tide. Staging toward Zehas or Titan rumors often begins with harbor syndicates, Asnaes credit lines, and captains who rename ships between moorings.

Zehas volcanic arc

Zehas anchors ashfall seasons—vineyards gamble on fertility while lungs gamble on masks. Priest-geologists debate whether eruptions negotiate with Storm shrines or deeper Titan dreams.

Highfold Titan stairs

Switchback shrines argue whether peaks are sculptures or sentences; genius loci winds knit tourists’ hair into temporary braids locals charge to photograph. Relic ossuaries tempt scholars who should know better.

Fiadh March, Duinn Sléibhe & trading stairs

Travelers’ songs name Fiadh March—mist border cantons where watchers favor wolves over poetry. Duinn Sléibhe barrows loom on shepherd maps as hills that rehearse verse without permission.

In Collimar’s mercantile heights, the Stilicho Exchange marble arcade imports opera budgets with spice futures; southern caravans whisper of Vishran Bazaar stairs—spice tiers where silence sells next to cardamom.
