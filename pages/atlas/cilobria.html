Overview

Cilobria reads like geometry homework completed by geniuses who despise curves. Warm interior seas calm barges; charter cities sync clocks so precisely that festivals end on shared seconds—locals insist this is harmony; visitors insist it is rehearsal for something louder.

Lore tied to older “Republic” expedition maps lands here—the artifact mindset stays: coastlines simplified until politics fits on one sheet.

For how Cilobria sits on Eldarum’s oceans and neighbors, read World of Eldarum; for weekdays, seasons, and trade tempo canal barges argue over, see Calendar, weather & trade.

The Cilobrian Republic

Power splits between a rotating Senate of Land and a Maritime Syndicate whose admirals wear civilian waistcoats to pretend commerce leads war. Laws export neatly on vellum; revolutions import messily on handbills shipped from Asceveron water-stained but enthusiastic.

Senate Spire of Varn

A glass dome debates echo policies until populists cough theatrically. Lobbyists train birds to drop amendments—symbolism illegal only when someone films.

Canal Ledger District

Tax barges glide pulled by choir-trained horses—according to brochures. Accountants punt between embassies; ghosts of older compacts cling to mooring rings.

Metric Glassworks

Standard lenses for standard lies—windows display identical sunsets across provinces so citizens feel simultaneously patriotic.

Eastern express rails

Lines sprint toward frontiers engineers deny emotionally—ticket inspectors double as folklorists collecting monster gossip from cargo.

Museum of Imperial Compacts

Treaties hang as art thieves steal definitions instead of canvases—black markets trade comma placements.

Titan strata under civic stone

Titan Wars relics lie beneath foundations—excavation permits require three hymns and notarized apologies. Genius loci manifest as humming tramlines; commuters complain about songs stuck in infrastructure.

Trade with Asceveron

Grain futures travel east; precision manufactures travel west; poets travel everywhere broke. Stammont merchants joke Cilobrian scales weigh souls—Republic merchants reply souls should be standardized anyway.
