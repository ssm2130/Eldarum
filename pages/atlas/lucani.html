Overview

West of Asceveron, mirror-blue water shatters into archipelagos whose cartographers retire young. Navigation blends reef-lore, lantern grammar, and bribes paid to currents themselves—pilots swear some channels remember insults.

Floating bastions such as Celxis drift above storm stacks where Nexites sell passage between sky-isles for handfuls of gold per day or month; dragonborn alchemists host delegations because Lucani refuses to separate commerce from miracle. Inland marches like Boxguay Woods still hide blighted towns where spider courts negotiate with warlocks who eat starlight—threads usually filed under campaign chronicles rather than polite atlas margins.

For ocean-scale framing and archipelago neighbors, read World of Eldarum; for seasons and trade winds that set departure windows, see Calendar, weather & trade.

Kazon & free harbors

Kazon (also profiled from Northwake voyages) exemplifies Lucani politics: jurisdictions rented by the tide, syndicates auctioning neutral flags, captains renaming hulls between moorings to spite creditors. Houses like Asnaes underwrite manifests honest only on alternate weeks. When volcano gods or titan spiders enter the itinerary, crews stage here last so ballast can be swapped for clerics.

Greywake economics

Corsairs rebrand as couriers when crowns need deniability; privateers collect medals when crowns need witnesses. Smuggling dialects fuse Zrelian admiralty slang with Stammont contract ink—lawyers island-hop chasing precedent like sport fish.

Storm Saint skerries

Rock chapels seize lightning into benedictions scorched into stone—worshippers wear singed scarves as seasonal fashion. Storm Court chaplain-boats tie beside taverns serving thunder-clap rum.

Coral notary rings

Reefs host contracts barnacle-sealed; breaking a promise allegedly raises tides against your grandchildren—tourists buy miniature reefs as souvenirs.

Kelp scriptoriums

Archives woven underwater demand readers hold breath and grudges—monks export illuminated algae banned in Cilobrian museums for excessive sincerity.

Chartwright guilds

Maps sell with apology appendices; competing guilds feud through cartographic gossip—sandbars migrate when reputations sour.

Shards & strange beaches

Travelers chasing shard lore—whether warlocks consuming them or historians trying to bury them—hear beaches hum at dusk when Lucani tides align. Cloudcrest’s old dig sites, Adla’s blighted treelines, and Kethtek Marghull’s workshops on Celxis each tell part of that story in the campaign record: read the continent’s atlas for geography, then follow Campaign journal when you need the moral weather of those searches.
