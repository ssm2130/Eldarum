Baycliff

Wind-scoured harbor notorious for watchers and whiskey.

Baycliff’s terraces cling to cliffs like stubborn lichen—every staircase argues with gravity. Customs houses sport more knives than pens; signal lamps wink coded gossip to ships riding offshore. Intelligence contacts sometimes name Ian McCreaty among reliable listeners for crown agents rotating through Asceveron postings—including couriers such as Ronan MacGilroy, whose Hazoret-field brief treats McCreaty as first harbor handshake.

Economy & vice

Pearl sorting, smoked squid, and discreet banking occupy daylight; gambling dens borrow tides for metaphors about luck. Foreign captains complain Baycliff measures distances in betrayals per league. Merchant Navy passages south often stage through Hazelshire or Wilden before Stammont landfall, which is why quartermasters here speak three currencies before breakfast.

Contacts, taverns, and cover stories

Intelligence tradecraft still treats the Candlelight Tavern as neutral ground—sailcloth on the walls, booths low enough for tricorn hats to vanish. Field briefs name Hermes McCaw: maroon tricorn, blue feather, phrases about fair winds as prearranged handshake. Merchant Navy assets fresh from Sverge debark with aquamarine coin enough for quiet berths while they chase Roafort weddings or Darkshore admirals; those same sailors may never know they share benches with Zrelian cartographers pretending neutrality.

Listeners & lantern grammar

Signal lamps do not only warn ships—they auction gossip to whoever pays pilot guilds in Collimar salt or Zrelian citrus. Crown “listeners” compare dreams to tide tables; experienced agents track which taverns salt their beer heavier during succession freezes. Intelligence briefings name codes stolen from Darkshore drills and Kazon truce weeks alike; Ronan-shaped plots treat Baycliff as the hinge between seaboard rumor and inland ledger ink.
