Relics of the Scourge — named roster

The Roll20 export threads dozens of relics through Dormant, Awakened, and Exalted beats. At the table, each name is more than loot: Aetherius paces ranged volleys like a liturgy; Rak Bal'uroth teaches that warhammers can hunger; Bl'ethea blurs lock and blade until doors become arguments. Pyrravyn waits in fragments that story text ties to Forge of Rasdon and Mount Cragatain; Conqueror's Eye tutors casters through an orb focus; Crimson Devourer gloves carry Vampire Lord resonance; bardic Naeryndam accrues languages like ballads; Virtue rewards unbent alignments with radiant summons; Yamruck cinches vitality and poison drama at the belt. The concise catalog with cross-links to cosmology lives on Magic items; verbatim tier text and DCs remain in the full archive search.

Mechanical warning carried in the journal: Relic attunement can demand a Constitution 18 gate or slap exhaustion when pride outruns physiology—especially called out around Conqueror's Eye commentary.

Magic & lore threads

Abrakary surfaces as a ghost curriculum predating Magi-Bane—spell names like Chicanerous Semblance and Planar Void ride alongside instability tables that punish greedy casters. That lineage matters because Nila's silence scrubbed whole schools for centuries; every Abrakary page recovered is an argument with divine editorial policy. Night's Edge speaks with an Astral dreadnought's leftover grammar; Nicrolith Mantle turns doors into lunar puzzles worth session prep. Alongside those epic runners sit practical toys—Deck of Illusions, Apparatus of Mastery, Zorleon's Handy Robe—mapped to specific PCs so gifts feel personal rather than random treasure.

World — calendar & environment

Campaign play anchors in year 1,559 AB, with snapshots such as 10th Bromdel occasionally annotated beside real-world dates for the GM's notebook—always privilege the in-world year when wiki and session disagree. Nine months rotate through named seasons (Straral, Juhna, Tizuli, Jorus among them) with weekday cadences (Thorus through Neura) and obsessive weather tables in the export: those tables are how smog, ash, and ship delay become diegetic truth. Twin suns Vils and Vols share sky with moons Raral, Tivoli, Honus—ties that matter when lunar locks (such as astral ruins near Greenveil) appear in both journal prose and Pantheon Titanic Records.

People, factions & locales (highlights)

Admiral Craxton's correspondence berths siege psychology—ballistae math, chain-shot rehearsal—because Darkshore expects Zrelian hulls where polite maps still pretend peace. Gnome engineer Ipvar “Bullseye” O'Poke trails Apparatus of Mastery procurement like comedy until paperwork proves otherwise. Smuggling ecology clusters around Elliswich Quag: Shepherds laundering caravans, Beggar's Hole swallowing witnesses, Bramble glamours rewriting witness memory, Thrail feuds rerouting revenge. Down-river in Collimar's underworld, Serpent Gang ledger ink still wet beside Shepherds "charity" fronts—Spiros Larue names, dock casinos, jobs smuggling contraband into either crown because neutrality pays double. Ash theology intersects Zehas, where hag bargains and cracked shrines litter exports until hazmat clerics intervene. Across Greywake, Lucani floats Celxis alchemy upward while Mooncreeper shipments chase Julian's cure arc. Northern Wilden districts anchor Sverge travelers whose census blocks live only inside the searchable journal.
