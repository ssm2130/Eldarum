Sources

This chronicle distills GM planning drafts and session outlines into readable prose. Where helpful, mechanics appear as asides rather than spreadsheet dumps; Roll20 handouts remain authoritative for stats and dated table facts.

Patron — Kul Buldar

Kul Buldar expects the crew to behave like more than sellswords. Standing orders emphasize outcomes that resemble a survey company with a grim expense account: map Titan-era remains before rival powers strip them; keep Captain Julian Welles breathing long enough for a real cure; stare into whatever opened at Zehas before someone worse does; reconcile Asnaes deliveries with what the ledgers promised; and protect trade routes toward Kazon (older itineraries named the traveler Sazi). Beneath those headings sit private bargains struck player by player.

Session zeros

Anoceth — merchant corsair turned spy

Anoceth returns from half a year at sea aboard the Merchant Navy brig Nightingale. His captain father, Thommas Auvray, brokers an audience with Admiral Garlet Bouchet, who sees the psychic daggers demonstration and scraps the mundane posting he had imagined. Bouchet lays out Republic-scale homework: infiltrate how Stammont and Zrelia actually move materiel when court heralds lie; catalog pirate captains by true names; map hidden harbors feeding grey markets; report face-to-face every nine months if possible; never hint at the posting to dockyard gossips.

The admiral presses an aquamarine coin—ship-and-wave relief—into Anoceth’s palm for discreet passage aboard Merchant Navy or Long Trading hulls. First contact ashore is Hermes McCaw in Baycliff’s Candlelight Tavern: maroon tricorn, blue feather, passphrase Fair day to ya and a strong wind to fill the mains! Hermes routes him toward Roafort’s upcoming wedding where Admiral Craxton will toast his niece; follow-up intel comes from a Captain Grelax jawing loudly about Craxton’s timetable while treating his officers to late-night ale.

Parallel dossiers exist for Fiadh (two-book split), Flavius, Grax, Mere, and Ronan—each bundles calendar months, sensory detail, and street-level shopping grids you can mine whenever a session needs texture before the royal arc swallows everyone.

Court rumors & side plots

The Leister Territory Adventure paths based on Royal Rumors handout treats the pre-wedding salon as an index of darker modules. Guests trade whispers about a townsman blanked after a goblin jaunt—the plan ties that to Horrus Dune finishing a ‘project.’ Another guest envies treasure said to linger in an abandoned manor west of Shimmergate (The Haunt). Ravenfort remembers bandits who buried loot in a swamp now claimed by a young black dragon. A northern meteor stains everything touched with hypertrophied beasts. Imported gem dust links a quiet townhouse to an emergency teleport anchor between Roafort and Zrelian command; deep investigation traces the purchases to the basement circle. Vicecount Henchy supposedly bought geas-laced wines in Collimar to leash the Countess Farrington while embezzling through a ghost-killer plot that leaves Ravenfort’s cemetery restless. Parallel hooks involve anti-magic revolutionaries (The Veil), Pullac’s weighted coffins, golden songbirds that may be dragons molting feathers, and Gadarast’s personal fey bolt-hole.

The missing bride

Before the catastrophe, Leo Smith at Roafort’s Prancing Pony turns outstanding debts into narrative urgency: Allie vanished six days ago; the ceremony happens in three; seven hundred gold pieces plus cleared tabs await whoever brings her back alive; two Crimson Guard detachments already marched into silence; Leo insists every rumor credits him so debtors cannot vanish quietly.

The Red Wedding

The formal module walks GMs through crowd composition—court, merchants, dignitaries—then narrates Allie vomiting mid-oath, greening, swelling into the Quellion-shaped horror named in your stat block. During the clash, rupturing blisters aerosolize what scholars later label demon blisters. Noble kin, clergy, even the groom risk infection before anyone understands stacking dispels matter more than tonic.

The aftermath checklist asks who received gentle repose, who still breathes under quarantine, and how investigators layer detect disease with detect magic until someone realizes simultaneous countermagic and paired dispel magic plus lesser restoration—or a DC 25 short-stem staghorn brew—might peel the curse off living tissue.

War & coming home

Two days after psychic ripples disturb Mere’s sleep, whichever route home the party chooses lands them in wartime staging. Elven scouts ambush travelers near Faehodin if tensions spike; Roafort locks gates while masonry still settles from tremors unrelated to siege engines. Bulletins shout that Zrelia struck without warning—Darkshore naval duels, Tomb Bay crowded with burning hulls, Nortown contested, Sargues sacked—because someone threaded the Tempest Fury and emerged in Stammont’s northern marches. Admiral Craxton and General Hagen redeploy ahead of civic calm.

Riot doctrine follows fear: infected citizens who fully warped die by spear and pyre; exposed nobles named Allie Craxton, Owen McDonnell, Eimear McDonnell, Patrick Henchy endure sealed manors while the heroes already chase fungi that might spare them.

The cure

Session four becomes an investigative pilgrimage. Reece O’Hannon shepherds Obsidian Order access; the Chamber of Refinement’s silent monk fetches tomes only members may read. Investigation ladders climb from generic mushroom lore (DC 15) toward proof the contagion climbed from Underdark commerce (DC 25). Elion Keldan—elf alchemist tied to both ‘Red Rook’ and ‘Red Hook’ tavern names in notes—knows the short-stem staghorn cap must be refined from caverns northwest of Faehodin, roughly a hundred twenty miles through bandit prairies, owlbear groves, displacer packs, troll valleys, and (if fate jokes) velociraptor paddocks hidden behind limestone curtains.

Festival & the road east

Survivors steal joy where they can: first of Nogule honors Jorus with tankards, skewers, and arena bloodsport—Manius versus Tullus ends with severed limbs and bruised pride when well-meaning magic annoys the loser. Outfitting follows: horses, herbs, forged papers. The Bronze Rose in Toufort waits at the next river stop while Finnius Flax becomes both ally and temptation.

Toufort & the magistrate

Collacht jurisdiction reminds Flavius that swagger which worked in Leister earns irons here. Captain Rob Boan, Governor Bernaldo Sardinas, visiting General Freddie Hagen, and Magistrate Henry Jordan choreograph a public trial complete with scaffold optics. Finnius plays court spider—soft voice, Littlefinger polish—feeding slaver routes north into the swamp while bargaining for town support. Successful rhetoric earns deferred punishment plus crates of donated gear; Demnamli Orcarm’s feud with Thrail’s Oakentoes can even unlock returning daggers and gemmed leather if the party publicly shames that rival clan.

Elliswich & Dearil’s Keep

The Mire of Albermer spreads thirty miles north of Toufort across a twenty-five-mile square quilt of sucking peat. Thieves’ cant warnings hang from driftwood. Random routes slap travelers with bullywugs, ogre camps, troll bridges, or the dragon-sense prickle of an apex predator lounging in flooded treasure vaults. Dearil’s gatehouse sends sending stones to Lord Nyx; Lady Violet mirrors his cruelty. storming the keep rarely yields Darragh’s location—mind-controlled retainers, flying escapes, and encoded ledgers punish frontal assault—but Fiadh keeps hunting because love and duty share the same chain.

Beggar’s Hole

The hidden freehold proves that monstrous ancestries can build society if secrecy holds. Thorned illusion ends the road until someone pierces it; fifty camouflaged warriors rise from boards laid in reeds; Fiadh receives kin-welcome while humans taste suspicion. Githzerai, duergar, and firbolg elders host a feast laced with dust of deliciousness so potent that only DC 25 insight questions the hospitality. After dinner they explain modify-memory geas masquerading as ceremony: consent or resist at disadvantage, because Beggar’s Hole survives only if cartographers cannot betray its vectors.

Night of cards & dreams

The hag’s table deals mechanical boons—gems, attribute spikes, duelists’ vows—then nightfall hijacks sleep. Inye’s nightmare stages a duel to unconsciousness, a voice insisting she consume the purple shard until veins glow amethyst. Althinias wanders a hoard-king fantasy until gold chokes him awake. Mere receives tenderness with Katie, children of dusk-blue skin, breakfast warmth—then PURPOSE tears the earth open and buries the dream in ash.

Greenveil astral ruins

Mere and Julian glimpse cerulean flare through briars. Clearing fallen towers exposes paired lunar menhirs ignited only when Honus shows new and Raral hangs full—each alignment lights another constellation stone until a pillar spins open on a stair rigged with transmutation sigils that turn steps into slides unless fourth-circle abjuration lands true. Skeletons guard a zodiac wheel demanding five heroes stand on crystal plates while a fifth turns paired symbols (Hunter to Wolf, Maiden to Womb, onward through the sequence). Side chambers force banshee duets, poltergeist dodgeball, parkour over false floors, stealth across crystal tripwires, portable-hole mouse tricks, and a Kyratrix idol demanding chaotic offerings. The spiral ends where an Astral Devourer hunts Sazi the celestial raccoon; victory yields the Infinitous Codex and twin orbs that reopen surface stairs from either direction.

Into Kul Buldar

Fifty miles of Asnaes forest roll into Larn-Loch’s stilted docks where Abdalla mends nets and Ronum the Ashmaker still sketches contrails overhead. The dwarven seal reads like moral philosophy—only what belongs may pass—until someone presses dwarven-forged iron to stone. Beyond lies the Room of Kings: Pyrravyn resting beside platinum vaults, fire giants answering noise, goblin tides squeezing survivors toward a balor’s furnace gleam, then a lava bridge where arrows threaten to dunk climbers a hundred fifty feet into magma. Lost tunnels eventually exhale onto Kul Buldar’s terraced cavern-city and the brawling pit advertised as Beard beneath the Kilt, where milestones tick toward seventh level.

Horrus Dune returned

The second descent maps cult laboratories along lava veins—Kyratrix tattoos, blistered acolytes, trapped fiends used as batteries. Balhannoths and udaak-blocked chokepoints funnel toward Horrus himself juggling Hunger of Hadar, infernal summons, forcecages, and mad darkness until his wounded body folds through magenta fire. Loot ranges from soul-coins to planar rods; the gateway checks arcana against Uccasos bleed-through.

The mine crisis

Mythral Consortium coin buys patience while geothermal breaches threaten duergar galleries, sapphire caverns, and illithid bolt-holes tied to the same arch-warlock ecology—timers matter as much as CR.

Search for the shard

High-altitude survival rules punish speed choices: navigation DC 18, blizzards swallow stragglers, avalanches bury the unwary. Yeti-shaped druids led by Fionhala Keefei test intruders before acknowledging Zirrith’s rival claim on the peaks. Inside the white wyrm’s ice cathedral, kobold engineers rig piercers and snow golems; treasure lies frozen in strata requiring days of chipping unless spellfire buys expedience. Dusk Song waits among Lucani-scale hoard coins. Beyond lies glacier lakes stalked by remorhazes, frost giant camps guarding star metal and Mooncreeper vital to Julian’s longer cure arc, and a titan skull whose fractures demand coordinated skill checks to harvest another shard.

Motivations across the continent

By tier eight the sandbox competes for attention: Asnaes still owes coin; Kul buyers price star metal per pound; Emis schemes to bait Ronum; Zehas demands boots on ash; Kazon reminders echo Sazi’s old route; southern Sending spells hijack Anoceth with visions of Remi in pirate hands; Dortar Eterna sermons mirror Dhotarr cadence; Cenkhem’s transceiver quest forces Ronan into theft; shared nightmares depict Tomb Bay bridged by an earth titan until Inye’s void gullet swallows whole armies; Celxis schedules Kethtek Marghull’s consult for Julian’s next cure stage.

Narlow — turmoil in the Republic

Narlow sits inland on the Choral River, crown jewel of Sverge commerce. Five gentle houses—the Long Manner, Hallow Hill, Degalle, Demande, Hawk—traditionally guarantee stability through trade surplus. Lately inexplicable crime waves silence even convicted perpetrators. City posts invite professionals to Flame Heart Citadel where Gilbert Genest explains the bounty. Surface calm masks probe questions: what do imprisoned thieves refuse to say? why does Abeau Mahrdaki’s fortune spike within months? Follow money through Temple of Self desecrations, Ravenwood’s sealed lips, diamond parlors, waterfront warehouses stacked like Mahadi’s ledgers, Mahrdaki manor libraries hiding demon-bound ledgers, until a basement gate opens on the Crushing Depths where Malvolia’s court finishes the conspiracy.

The pirate incursion

Bloodtooth corsairs stage Dragontooth theatrics—Thessalkraken legends, dragon turtle escorts, island tombs—until the party storms Skull Mountain’s red halls, frees prisoners including Remi Forlette, and confronts courtiers drinking beside chained celestials.

Zehas volcano

A continent-wide tremor announces the ruin of Zehas’s harbor stacks. Survivors cluster in wooden grids while obsidian roads still aim toward lava tubes hot enough to demand initiative-tracked constitution saves. Firenewts, salamanders, elementals, and eventually Imix guard a planar throat into pure fire; sealing or failing the vent decides whether molten ruin slides toward the refugee camp or finally sleeps.

Lucani — Adla & Boxguay Woods

The town of Adla nestles deep inside Boxguay Woods. Once the forest buffered trade and gossip alike; now a creeping blight sickens trunks and minds together. Sap runs black as tar, wildlife flees, and villagers wake each morning closer to being surrounded. Adla cleared its interior trees for cropland, yet harvests thin and livestock starve in slow motion. Nobody smiles in the square anymore: Baldus Mats the woodworker looks twenty years beyond his age; Harbert and Ann Millson drag sacks between field and market stall as if each trip might be their last; young Alarise Haim keeps the ovens lit because routine is all that remains when hope thins.

Stepping into Boxguay feels like trespassing in a drowned cathedral. Canopy twilight never lifts; mud pulls at boots; bark weeps ichor. Rot sweetens the air until a hum vibrates in the sternum—more felt than heard—hinting that the forest is neither empty nor asleep. Birdsong died here long ago; shadows twitch at the corner of vision as if the gloom itself checks newcomers. Failed Wisdom saves lure travelers off the safe path before they realize fear has steered them.

Faint lights dance ahead. Anoceth reads them clearest: lantern-glow cupped by tentacle stalks close enough to touch before investigation proves the shimmer belongs to a Death Kiss camouflaged among dying boles—bait hung for anything hungry enough to ignore instinct.

Deeper still, stray silk strands become nets. Threads glitter sickly between trunks, then thicken until the party must tear through cobweb corridors with Athletics contests while Perception races to spot giant spiders before ambush. The mesh muffles sound as if the forest holds its breath; every step squelches until the air itself feels viscous. Constitution checks threaten nausea from the stench; another wave of checks raises impossible egg sacs overhead—each orb large enough to swallow a rider, some twitching with inner motion—while Wisdom saves struggle against existential dread. Only a near-impossible Perception contest pierces Eastra’s camouflage until she chooses to be seen.

Then the voice arrives without direction: it praises survival, admits curiosity about their thoughts, and names Inye as the guest it requires in private council—histories past, present, and future offered in exchange for brokering that meeting, while any violence meets immediate reprisal and captives in silk face Intelligence saves against mental intrusion each round.

Only after the ultimatum does Eastra manifest as the Arasta terror—chitin lacquered in shadow, lesser spiders mantling her abdomen, limbs folding through angles that insult geometry, hair unraveling into living tripwires. When the scene demands apocalyptic scale she rises as the Titan Spider: countless eyes catching moonlight like wet glass; mandibles ticking out a rhythm that shakes the web; jaws opening on black ichor while warmth leeches from the clearing. Her Armor of Spiders narrative beat is a shriek like slate scored by a thousand carapaces, followed by a tide of little spiders armoring her bulk. She claims kinship with Fomysus, goddess of disease and misfortune, and speaks of gods, titans, and the scar Inye’s patron tears in reality. Mind-delving reveals names Eastra lacked moments before; she promises cosmic intelligence if Inye—or the secrets she carries—returns to negotiate on honest terrain.

Floating Lukani & Kethtek Marghull

Lucani’s floating isles drift above storm seas; Nexites at Ixus Square broker teleport pads between islands—five gold one way, eight round trip, fifteen daily, fifty weekly, one hundred seventy-five monthly, annual passes by private negotiation—so adventurers without skyships can still chase leads across Celxis and Ilrac alike. Guides such as Upud Kilturim greet travelers beside desk attendants Yunxas Caerrinn and a roster of Nexite names logged for continuity with your tabletop notes.

Near Adla the frontier between civilization and myth blurs enough that theologians whisper whether Eastra qualifies as surviving godstuff rather than mere megafauna.

On Celxis, silver-scaled dragonborn alchemist Kethtek Marghull prepares to meet allies who track the same titan tumult. He lodges the party at the Gorgeous Garden—room, board, and bath included—while handing over a seven-day treatise titled Alchemist Shortcut. Finishing it permanently halves brewing time and cost for potions and elixirs, cementing him as Julian’s cure pipeline long after the woods quiet.

Tracking Inye

Clues scatter across libraries and spy nets. Highrock’s stacks hold shard research yet deny circle access; head librarian Thomas Hilless still answers polite questions. Collimar’s Grand Archive staff—Zachary Wise, Aubrey Trivett, Humphry Mallin—cross-reference mountainous topography until “Cloudcrest” surfaces as the cradle where early scholars harvested the first shards before the site went abandoned, leaving only haunted foundations and stray echoes of the ancients.

Field intelligence says Inye recently ghosted through Highrock while relying on eight-hour etherealness jaunts, arcane gate choreography, and a helm of teleportation—making her slippery enough to chase across Highrock, Collimar, Thrail, Castle Ghealach, even Mekhit Wadi tombs if clues pile high enough. Fiadh’s arena persona Korga Bloodfury intersects the hunt: upgrades for Dearil assets, barrier-breaking schemes, shadow-plane ingress where mundane walls negate magic, and paired gladiators covering her flanks.

Ronan messages Ian McCreaty: Eastra near Adla admitted to being a living titan and demanded guidance on Hazoret’s expectations; Immes—an ancient green dragon once treated as antagonist—surfaces as possible ally because Eastra spoke the name aloud. Duergar mentor Darragh Stormforge remains vital: scarred, band-marked, proud of Fiadh’s kills, grieving Rolland, desperate to reunite father and daughter even if mind control scarred their bond.

Bridge to tier fourteen

After the audience, Eastra refuses tidy exits: deliver Inye willingly or remain entangled until the bargain completes. Ronan and Althinias reach for sending; Inye agrees to parley and names Highrock in Sverge—Althinias stiffens because hometown politics and divine scrutiny converge there. Eastra also named Immes, an ancient green dragon the party never filed under “ally,” forcing you to reconcile prior loot or favors tied to that wyrm.

Before sessions resume, the plan document urges deeper deity answers (Formoria, Inye’s patron), richer Highrock NPC texture, refreshed Inye statistics, and a rehearsed sequence for Eastra’s second summit once Inye arrives.

Pact of Valenore

The infernal contract promises otherworldly luck steering wealth and power until death, provided the signatory’s soul slides straight to Valanore afterward—silence enforced by cosmic lien, breach clauses harsher than bankruptcy.
