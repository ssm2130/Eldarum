Collimar

Capital of Stammont: bells, banners, and budgets that bleed.

Collimar rises where three rivers braid into a harbor broad enough to swallow rhetoric. Palaces of pale limestone overlook markets roofed in colored tile; beneath both run cisterns older than the crown and tunnels whose maps are sealed by decree. Physicians, miracle mongers, and curse-breakers crowd the arcade districts—soldiers discharged from frontier campaigns sometimes linger here seeking diagnoses royal clerics refuse to ink. Broker-monks of the Obsidian Order cart folios between ministries; one field cartographer raised in their vaults now walks the kingdom’s roads as Vishra-Nari.

Government district

The Crown Lattice keeps ministries for grain, tides, smoke, and sorrow—each housed in towers linked by skybridges officials claim improve ventilation and critics call convenient for discreet falls. Succession lawyers outnumber poets; both drink in the same taverns and quote contradictory precedents.

Daily life

Dawn bell choirs synchronize across parishes; midday belongs to harbor cranes and lawsuits; dusk belongs to lantern barges selling fried kelp and gossip. Crime syndicates mimic guild charters; honest thieves carry stamped receipts ironically forged better than royal seals.

Notable services

  • War hospitals: Treat veterans from Darkshore campaigns; experimental cures draw scholars and quacks.
  • Harbor pilots: Navigate Mirror Reach currents; unofficially navigate politics.
  • Archives: Hold Three-Crown Compact originals—reading rooms smell like vinegar and anxiety.

Beneath the Lattice — docks, casinos, syndicates

Crown ministries float above cobbles; cellars negotiate without charters. Harbor tales speak of Shepherd fronts packing wool that smells like ink, Serpent collectors who prefer ledgers to throats, and jobs smuggling liquor north toward Minwall or outward to admiralties that deny receiving them.
