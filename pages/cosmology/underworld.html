Overview

The Underworld is a parasitic reflection plane that overlays the Material Plane like a malignant echo. Every structure, forest, and road exists here — but degraded, hollowed, and collapsing under relentless entropy. Time flows unevenly, decay accelerates, and life is reduced to its most fragile, failing state.

When a creature crosses into the Underworld, they arrive at the exact mirrored location they left — only to find it:

  • Darkened beneath a sickly green sky
  • Choked with black, pus-filled tendrils
  • Saturated with the smell of rot, mildew, and death

This is not merely a shadow — it is what the world would become if abandoned to decay.

Environmental characteristics

The mirrored world

Every location exists as a degraded duplicate:

  • Cities become ruined husks
  • Forests become skeletal groves of blackened trees
  • Rivers stagnate into thick, tar-like sludge

The green sky

A permanent, dim green haze: no visible sun or stars; light is diffuse and sickly; shadows stretch unnaturally.

Tendril growths

Black, semi-organic structures pulse slowly as if alive; they secrete foul-smelling fluids and react subtly to movement and emotion. These tendrils act as a quasi-nervous system for the plane itself.

Psychological effects on visitors

Creatures in the Underworld experience progressive deterioration:

  1. Disquiet — unease, paranoia, heightened senses
  2. Despair — emotional numbness, lethargy
  3. Erosion — memory lapses, identity weakening
  4. Assimilation — becoming part of the plane

Long-term exposure risks turning a creature into a native entity.

Throne & jurisdiction

In one influential chronicle, the plane answers to Famera, Queen of the Underworld — once known as Ala, messenger of death, who exploited mass death during the Titan Wars to harvest souls beyond mandate and was bound to rule the decay she amplified. Whether your Eldarum canon uses that mask verbatim, or distributes custody across Silent Arbiter archives and lower moral courts, keep sovereignty negotiated, not scenic.

Native manifestations

These entities are not merely inhabitants — they are manifestations of decay and reflection. Names shift by saga; shapes do not.

Gravehowl stalker
Pack hunters tracking fear and distress; bodies flicker between solid and shadow; echo howls disorient prey.
Mireborn colossus
Towering slow horrors of fused organic matter and tendrils; absorb terrain as they move; drawn to concentrations of life.
The Hollow Warden
Tall, thin humanoid stalker with featureless face; appears near emotionally charged places.
Dreadmare of the Veil
Horse-high at the shoulder only in the insult of perspective — distances lie here. Canonical accounts describe a thing wreathed in black vapor, hoofprints that leave corridors of accelerated decay, and mounts that herald Famera when she chooses theater over statute. Sometimes it slips the border for breaths at a time: not invasion, advertisement.

Points of interest

  • The Withered Mirror — boundary thin enough that reflections move independently; travelers risk replacement by Underworld counterparts.
  • The Rotspire — spiraling tower of tendrils; pulses influence weather across adjacent scar-terrain.
  • The Silent Fields — collapsed structures across vast plains where sound fails; thoughts feel muted.

Narrative pressures

Use the Underworld as:

  • a parallel horror expedition — same geography as home, morally inverted;
  • a risked shortcut between crises (time and rot need not bargain fairly);
  • a corruption dial — characters who linger trade clarity for affinity with native hungers;
  • a court of last appeals — Famera’s pacts tempt the desperate while indexing their failures.

Typical pressures on stories: reflections begin moving before bodies do; dignitaries discover Underworld duplicates wearing their decisions; villages taste rot because a tendril milestone reached their reflection first.

Core theme

The Underworld is not just death — it is the inevitability of decay given form. It answers one question: what remains when life, memory, and meaning are stripped away?
