Daily Life & Customs

Bread, vows, festivals, and the choreography of coexistence.

Life in Eldarum oscillates between festival and famine with stubborn normality—people marry during harvest fairs even when admiralties threaten harbors; children learn three different songs for dawn depending on whether they live by river, sea, or volcano shadow.

Urban routines

Cities like Collimar organize days around bells—legal bells, prayer bells, harbor bells accidentally synchronized during storms to confuse pilots humorlessly. Markets stack scents: fried dough, ink, horse sweat, imported citrus betraying trade sanctions nobody admits signing.

Rural routines

Villages pace seasons by furrows and gossip—shepherds chart constellations renamed weekly by drunk uncles. Tithe collectors arrive with measuring cords blessed cheaply; Storm shrines leave barn doors open during lightning to “let judgment inspect debts.”

Festivals

Ash Homecoming follows Zehas ash eastward on peculiar winds: families sweep roofs together as symbolic forgiveness for whatever angered mountains this year—chimneys cough apologies; priests score the ritual by how evenly soot returns. Greywake Truce Week asks even bitter rivals to share kelp wine in designated harbors; assassinations become uncouth unless accompanied by jazz, which is how Kazon hears “culture.” Compact Eve turns jurisprudence into street theater—lawyers recite simplified Three-Crown clauses while children throw walnuts at inaccurate clauses, planting civic skepticism early.

Etiquette

Gifts cross palms wrapped in colored cloth signaling intent—blue for debt, green for alliance, black for “listen carefully.” Refusing tea twice accepts friendship; refusing thrice declares vendetta or comedy depending on tone.

Calendar literacy — whose year counts

Universities teach AB tallies pegged to Binding-era compacts; villages mix those figures with weekday rounds (Thorus–Neura) and harvest seasons named for winds rather than senators. Foreign traders from Cilobria import metric jokes; Lucani pilots import tide saints—everyone argues politely until payday.

Campaign journals fix “today” at 1559 AB alongside festival months like Bromdel; cosmology still flows from the Common Pantheon tome. If two festivals collide, consult Lore & primary sources before flipping a coin.
