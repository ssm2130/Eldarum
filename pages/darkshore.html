Darkshore

Fortified littoral where Stammont rehearses tomorrow’s naval war.

Darkshore is less a city than a habit of vigilance: layered batteries, signal towers, and barracks wedged between cliffs like teeth. Soldiers drill in surf-slick armor; chaplains bless cannon as if irony were ordinance. Raids from Zrelian corsairs—or independent wolves flying false colors—keep civilians fluent in evacuation drills.

Military infrastructure

Harbor chains rise at dusk; pilots memorize lantern codes that change with moon phase. Spy craft masquerades as sail repair or tavern violinists Baycliff couriers allegedly coordinate networks inland.

Civilian life

Fishers resent ballast dumped by warships; widows rent rooms to veterans writing memoirs nobody buys. Storm Court chapels double as storm shelters—the theological overlap is intentional.

War logistics in the mid-1500s AB

Coastal crowns press for siege trains—ballistae, stone shot, chain rounds—to answer admiralties who rehearse hull tactics nightly. When open war ignites between Stammont and Zrelia, Tomb Bay becomes a lantern-lit abattoir: hulls traded broadsides within sight of cliffs, admirals signing orders their heralds can barely read through smoke. Zrelian captains who learn the Tempest Fury’s moods can swing south of the usual patrol boxes, which is how northern marches suddenly learn invasion is not theoretical.

Chronicle-level detail on fleet movements, wedding-timed redeployments, and quarantine logistics lives in the Campaign journal; this article stays gazetteer voice while deferring dates to Roll20 exports whenever they differ.
