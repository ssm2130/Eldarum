Black Tusk Tribe

Orcish coalition staining Timberwild shadows.

The Black Tusk confederation paints tusks with ash and berry dye—marks outsiders misread as fashion until they learn each hue maps a season of raids. They prey on supply trains and isolated fortifications, coordinating drums with migratory bird calls stolen from border rangers who underestimated ornithology as warfare.

Leadership

Chieftain Nok’tar leads by momentum—wars begin because winter stores must move, treaties end because rivers argue with bridges. Shaman Lag’ra anchors spiritual terror: curses finger-pointed into barracks gossip long after wounds close. The warrior Khartag choreographs elite ambushes intimate enough to bruise morale beyond casualty counts; see the shared dossier when you need names attached to nightmares. Field lore connects Lag’ra’s doomed union with trader Rolland to Fiadh Stormforge, raised far from Timberwild drums but carrying proof of tusks in her ornaments.

Tactics

Night raids ride ridge fog the way chapels ride incense—same patience, different hymn. Feints mimic royal bell codes learned from captured camp followers; soldiers swing at ghosts while real tusks close from dry streambeds. Psychological warfare follows shamans into sick tents where hexes outlast physic—some veterans swear wounds ache seasons later because Lag’ra taught skin to remember fear as weather.

The Marauders Company paid for underestimating this theater near Towfort; campaign notes treat the clash as both tactical lesson and omen when crown logistics pretend geography is neutral.
