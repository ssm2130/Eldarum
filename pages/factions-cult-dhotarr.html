Cult of Dhotarr

Ink-black devotion threading high halls and low sewers.

Devotees of Dhotarr celebrate endings without funerals—contracts voided, names shelved, witnesses persuaded into convenient silences. Publicly dismissed as gutter myth, the cult nevertheless appears wherever budgets smell like fear: privatized prisons, lottery houses, academy archives with doors too expensive to audit.

Doctrine

Dhotarr’s creed treats secrecy as sacrament and debt as devotion. Initiates learn breathing patterns that mimic whispering galleries; archivists complain of palimpsests rewriting themselves after cult visitations—likely hyperbole until it isn’t.

Known objectives

Cells borrow heraldry to walk succession councils as honored cousins, trading legitimacy for days until seals cool. Harvesters treat curses as fungible grief—some black markets trade hexes like spices because Dhotarr brokers taught them how to standardize potency. When rivals must disappear, the cult prefers paperwork that ages into statute instead of knives that leave daylight blood—assassination without a corpse anyone can calendar.

Hunters of evil—wandering paladins and parish justiciars—prioritize Dhotarr alongside mundane bandits (notable persons). Early outlines tie Althinias’s arc to hunting this heresy through Sverge fronts and noble salons; beats are threaded narratively on the Campaign journal.
