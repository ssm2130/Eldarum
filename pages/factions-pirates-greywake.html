Greywake Corsairs

Sea wolves who negotiate with tides and treasuries alike.

Neither purely criminal nor purely patriotic, Greywake corsairs ride ambiguous commissions—Stammont letters of marque signed in disappearing ink, Zrelian admiralty pins traded for rum, independent captains who swear on compass needles blessed in storm chapels because secular charts lie politely about reefs.

Organization

Fleets fragment into squadrons named after weather—“Black Squall,” “Copper Mirage”—so admiralties argue about taxonomy while harbors refill shot lockers. Intelligence networks prize corsair watering holes when planning sanctions; failure to update charts costs commanders more than hulls because insurance guilds price ignorance as courage.

Campaign sea floor

Planning extracts describe emboldened piracy south of the Sverge Republic: messengers arrive breathless, black smoke visible to whole decks, captives traded for leverage in arcs that intersect Lucani-bound shipping and Kazon hearings when jurisdiction itself becomes cargo. Those beats belong to session logistics—dates and names stay authoritative in the Roll20 archive, while motives and fleet psychology stay narrated on the Campaign journal.

Player-facing hooks

Identify strongholds hidden behind fishing cooperatives where nets mend faster than ledgers. Trace markets where stolen crowns become melted buttons sold as “river tin.” Broker truces over kelp wine in neutral harbors where even admirals admit no flag owns the tide.

Dragon’s Tooth Lagoon

Greywake gossip treats Dragon’s Tooth Lagoon as both anchorage and moral punchline—a pyrate settlement where hulls hide inside mangrove teeth and captains swear the tide owes them rent. When mainland wars throttled grain and herds, Captain Brellon’s crews ran grain and livestock convoys inland until the lagoon ate famine instead of blame; broadsheets called him butcher, locals called him last winter’s uncle. Tales still pair Brellon’s silhouette with Grax, the lizardfolk muscle who made surrender speeches stick—finger bones spat rings, admiralty nightmares walked on two legs.

Three-ship naval sweeps eventually cooked Brellon’s flagship alongside a prize hull; surviving wreckage drifted myth-ward while buried chests kept financing Greywake hearsay.
