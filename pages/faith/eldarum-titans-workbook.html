Core interpretation

Primordial manifestations of divine will.

  • The gods did not physically enter Eldarum.
  • They sent avatars.
  • Those avatars were powerful enough to reshape continents, collapse civilizations, and tear planar boundaries.

Conclusion: mortals later mythologized these avatars as Titans.

What is a Titan?

A Titan is:

  • A fully manifested fragment of a god’s essence.
  • Anchored in the Material Plane during the Celestial Alignment.
  • Sustained by outer planar energy leakage.

They are not mere servants. They are semi-autonomous, capable of independent strategy, and embodiments of pure domain expression.

Why the Titans appeared

During the Celestial Alignment:

  • The veil thinned.
  • Outer planes bled into reality.
  • Gods could exert direct influence.

However, full divine manifestation would destroy the plane. Powers therefore forged Titans as controlled vessels.

Titan classification system

1. Prime Titans (god-direct avatars)

Main combatants of the Titan Wars.

Characteristics:

  • Size: mountain-scale.
  • Intelligence: godlike.
  • Power source: direct divine channel.
  • Death result: major shards.

Verdant Cataclysm (Nila’s Titan)

  • A living storm of roots, stone, and lightning.
  • Could overwrite terrain instantly.
  • Responsible for ending the war.
  • Important: Nila did not send a single avatar — she deployed multiple synchronized Titans.

The Iron Arbiter (Iocetis-aligned)

  • Construct-like entity of law magic and geometric energy.
  • Tried to stabilize reality mid-war.
  • Created regions of frozen time.

The Chaos Maw (Uccasos-aligned)

  • Ever-mutating form; constantly rewriting its own structure.
  • Where it moved: physics broke down; landscapes liquefied.

2. Echo Titans (residual manifestations)

When Prime Titans were destroyed, energy did not dissipate cleanly — it coalesced into unstable remnants: Echo Titans.

Traits: smaller (castle to city-sized), erratic, bound to specific regions.

Campaign use: boss-tier threats; guardians of major shard sites.

3. Shardbound Titans (post-war)

Mortals found shards and attempted to use them — producing artificial Titans via ritual and fusion. Often unstable or corrupted; power varies wildly.

The Scourge — Titan cascade collapse

The clash of nature-aligned avatars with rivals caused catastrophic destruction. Refined: when Nila intervened, her Titans did not merely defeat others — they forced simultaneous destruction, causing chain-reaction detonations, reality fractures, shards, planar scars, and possibly Underworld bleed-through points.

Connection to the Underworld

During Titan deaths, massive entropy was released; negative planes such as Dritora / Zaezirus bled through.

Theory (campaign use): the Underworld formed where Titan death-energy saturated reality — explaining mirrored geography, decay, and a failed version of reality.

Titan remains

Colossal corpses

  • Mountain-sized remains still radiating magic.
  • Often mistaken for natural formations.

Impact zones

  • Craters of wild magic or anti-magic (ties to Magi-Bane).

Embedded Titans

Some remain half-buried, dormant, or still alive.

The Magi-Bane (expanded)

Refinement of “magic disappeared after the war”: Titan destruction created arcane saturation collapse — too much divine energy caused magical burnout; magic shut down temporarily.

Factions & adventure hooks

Shard Seekers

  • Believe Titans were divine gifts; seek to reconstruct them.

Keepers of Balance

  • Hide or destroy shards to prevent another Titan War.

Underworld cults (Famera tie-in)

  • Worship the decay of Titans; treat entropy as the true end state.

Hooks: buried Titan pulses again; shard creates an artificial Titan; region shifts toward the Underworld; cult tries to reassemble a Prime.

Key theme

Titans are unchecked divine intervention translated into physical reality — not merely monsters, but weapons, mistakes, and warnings.

5e-compatible toolkit — Prime Titan

CR 26–30 equivalent. Templates to skin with names from your spreadsheet.

Core profile

  • HP: 600–900 · AC: 20–24 · Speed: 40 ft (or terrain-adaptive).
  • Saves: all proficient.
  • Resistances: nonmagical; elemental (by domain).
  • Immunities: poison; necrotic or radiant (by origin).

Titan traits

Divine mass: cannot be knocked prone, restrained, or grappled by creatures smaller than Gargantuan.

Reality pressure (aura, 120 ft): enemies suffer disadvantage on concentration; casting may require a CON save (DC 18–22) or fail.

Domain manifestation (examples): Nature → terrain reshapes each round; Law → initiative order fixed; Chaos → random-effects table each turn; Death → HP maximum reduction aura.

Multi-phase existence: at 75% / 50% / 25% HP — new abilities, dramatic terrain shifts, increased action economy.

Actions

Multiattack (3–5 attacks): baseline 25–40 damage plus rider (push, decay, burn, warp).

Cataclysm ability (recharge 5–6): terrain-wide effects (300+ ft); DEX saves vs collapse; CON saves vs corruption.

Legendary actions (3/round): move without provoking; attack; trigger domain effect.

Mythic trait (optional): at 0 HP — explode (Scourge echo) or return at 50% HP in altered form.

5e-compatible toolkit — Echo Titan

CR 17–22 equivalent.

Core profile

  • HP: 250–400 · AC: 17–20 · Speed: 30 ft.

Traits

Unstable form: each turn roll d6 — 1–2 lose AC; 3–4 damage boost; 5–6 AoE pulse.

Shard core: weak point / called shots; destroying it may trigger an explosion.

Actions

2–3 attacks per turn; one AoE every 2–3 rounds.

Death effect

Leaves a shard (artifact hook) and/or an environmental hazard zone.

Living Titan dungeon — “The Entombed Titan”

The dungeon is the body of a fallen or dormant Titan.

Zones

  1. Outer husk: stone-like skin, cracked plates; falling debris; residual magic bursts.
  2. Circulatory veins: glowing channels (“rivers of magic”); traverse by timing surges.
  3. Neural caverns: thought-echo zones; memories of the Titan War; psychic damage risk.
  4. Organ chambers: Heart Core (pulse = lair actions); Lung Vault (gas cycles); Stomach Pit (dissolution / entropy).
  5. Core chamber: dormant Titan wakes (Prime-lite); Echo emerges; or shard overload event.

Mechanics & rewards

Living environment: reacts to damage, noise, magic — walls close, veins reroute, gravity shifts.

Rewards: shards (minor/major), Titan-infused gear, permanent boons at a cost.

Titan impact sites (world integration)

Use with named Titans from your master spreadsheet — domain, death type, and shard presence determine map features.

Impact site types

1. Annihilation craters: no stable magic; wild magic surges; often mistaken for cursed lands.

2. Titanfall mountains: corpses become terrain — spine as range; skull as fortress or dungeon.

3. Reality fracture zones: thin planar boundaries; Underworld bleed; doppelgangers; temporal echoes.

4. Verdant reclamation (Nila): aggressive regrowth; hyper-evolving ecosystems — dangerous but fertile.

5. Dead magic regions (Magi-Bane scars): magic fails or behaves erratically; overload during Titan deaths.
