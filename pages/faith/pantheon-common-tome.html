Epistemic preamble

Much of the information contained in this tome is speculation, rumor, and conjecture. Over centuries, Acolytes, Anthropologists, Scholars, and Clerics collected various tomes, scrolls, and engravings to assemble this understanding of the Gods. Through casting spells such as Commune, some of the information contained within has been obtained or at least verified through interactions with the gods themselves.

Something that is always important to note when dealing with the gods is that they are Omnipotent beings. Due to the way they approach and perceive a question asked of them, the answers Clerics receive may be unclear. In some circumstances, the answers received from the gods may even wrong from what the cleric expected. These inconsistencies could be due to the way a god thinks or perceives the questions. The truth of some topics may be far beyond the mortals’ thinking or ability to understand complexity that only gods are capable of comprehending.

Often queries about the various alignments of gods receive responses that are unexpected. It is thought that this is because the gods do not view alignment in such simple terms as mortals do. What may be viewed as evil to mortals may be a necessary requirement for even a good god and vice-versa. Questions of this type are even more complicated to get clear answers on when asked of and about Neutral gods. Despite this, it tends to be easier to understand lawfulness versus chaos when querying the gods. But as with good and evil, neutral gods are much more difficult to label with these traits.

As much as we believe we know about the gods, it is extremely difficult to know how accurate the information is, what knowledge is missing, and what details are completely wrong or inaccurate. As mortals, we need to understand that we will never truly understand the motivations of the gods and we perhaps were never intended to. Many of the details provided within are a matter of faith and therefor do not necessarily have a basis in fact. Different races have different ways of worshiping the gods and those extricates also find themselves in the overall understanding of the gods.

Due to all of the above mention reasons, it is recommended that if you worship a particular god, to ask yourself what feels right to you. Your relationship with your god and the beliefs and faith you have in them may be varied from what you read here. Ultimately, your connection to the god and what they provide you is more important that the details described in a book.

A Brief History of the gods and Eldarum

Before the existence of anything, only a primordial intelligence existed without shape, form, or matter. Alone in the darkness, it ventured to create the basic matter of the Astral plane over an imperceivable amount of time. With the dust that floated in the immense space, the intelligence eventually took form and Aos was born. Over the course of Millions of years, Aos grew his power and control over the matter of the cosmos. As he began to manipulate this matter with this power, the first structures of the universe formed.

With no other life or intelligence in the cosmos, Aos decided to try to create a companion to build with. Aos broke a piece of his essence off of his corporeal form to seed the new being. Utilizing all of his power, he thought intently on creating a being that was nearly his equal. Over eons, eventually the mote of energy and godly matter joined with other matter and formed into another being. He named this creation Nila.

Aos’s efforts seemed to pay off as Nila was exactly the companion he was looking for. Nila took a completely different approach to creation and together they began to play with the basic elements of the cosmos. First the matter formed into increasingly larger chunks until eventually heat started to emanate in space. Not long after, the first star was born. Having created fire, they began to make other large groupings of matter that they referred to as “Earth”. Over time, experimenting with the matter and newly created object, they eventually formed their first comet, made mostly of ice. Together, they filled the cosmos with suns, comets, and meteoroids.

As these celestial bodies interacted with each other, the first water was discovered by Nila on one of the rocky meteoroids. She referred to the new creation as an Eldarum. With the heat of the sun, some of these Eldarums formed a gaseous material Nila referred to as Air. Nila seemed even more capable than Aos of creating these new features of the cosmos. Aos, therefore declared that Nila would have power over these creations. Before long, Nila referred to these creations as her offspring. Nila requested that Aos imbued them with power in an effort to allow them to spread on their own.

Aos was eager to please Nila agreed to her request. First, he used his breath to imbued the air with his godly power. The utilization of his power with Nila’s creation formed the first elemental god of wind Otila. Pleased with their creation, the pair set of to seed the cosmos with entities to control the base elements. They created Rasdon, the god of Fire and Brimstone to control the heat, fire, and power of the suns. Next, they created Edtris, the God of soil and stone. And finally, they imbued the liquid with the power of water, creating Amino, the god of water and the deep.

Pleased with their combined creation, they decided to take a rest and let these newly founded gods continue their creation. For millions of years, Otila, Rason, Edtris, and Amino expanded the universe as Aos and Nila watched while resting. Pleased with their creation, the universe took its form and was full of elemental power, but realized that something was missing. They looked to form the first influence that would seem to act on its own without direct control over its actions. Together, with a piece of Aos, they melded parts of Otila, Rason, and Amino to create something new. Toros was born as the first creation of life. Toros brought additional chaos to their creation. The Eldarums they created formed random forms of what they referred to as “Storms”. They decried that Toros would have the power over this new life.

After the creation of Toros, they thought there was more they could do to create this thing they called “life”. At this point Aos told Nila that he thought enough of his power has been spend to create new beings and he thought that with all of these elements in place that might spontaneously make new things. Nila agreed and while watching, they again rested, taking “The Great Rest”. Allowing their creation to act out their own will, the watched over millions of years as the cosmos again changed.

At some point in the future, Nila pointed at one of her Eldarums and noticed that it started changing color. Somehow the Eldarum took on a green hue. Happy with the progress, they continued to watch as the Eldarums began to form more and more “life”. After consulting with Otila, Rason, Edtris, and Amino, they realized that the combination of the elements was right to form these additional creations. Edtris told them they he worked with Amino and Toros to create the green living things. They realized the combination of these particular elemental gods allowed for unlimited creation.

As time went on, the creation exploded until various creatures began to exist. Surprised by this, Aos for a moment wanted to stop the progression as it seemed like it was out of control. Nila intervened and told Aos that it was too late to stop it and they should let it take its natural course. Before much longer, the first intelligent beings began to appear. These being diverged into various animalistic forms including humanoid races, dragons, and a seemingly unlimited number of various lifeforms.

As these various creatures populated the Eldarums, they began to fight for supremacy over the resources. From this, it became obvious to Aos that these creatures were out of control. While satiating Nila be letting them remain, he unilaterally decided that the best way to maintain control over these species was to create a new being that would help control their basest desires. He again took a piece of his own essence combined with matter of one of these beings and created Melandor, a god to have control over these beings. As Melandor came to existence, he began to exert his will over the beings. Trying to exert this force, he wiped out half of the life in the cosmos, working with Toros to exert is will.

Nila, unhappy with this new creation and how he utilized the powers of Toros, told Aos that she would like to have full control over Rason, Amino, Edtris, Otila. and Toros. She no longer wanted Melandor to have the ability to use these elemental gods to remove the life from the planet. Aos agreed to allow Nila control over these basis elements and she then referred to the elemental gods and the life created by them as “Nature”.

Regardless of Melandor having to relinquish the powers of Nature, the effect of his control had profound effects on the creatures living on these Eldarums. Some of the creatures began to worship him in an attempt for his will to provide them with food, luck, birth, and an immeasurable number of gifts. Other creatures saw the effects and realized the control it could have over the other creatures. Utilizing the fear produced by Melandors rule, they used this fear to terrorize the others. Melandor, realizing that he has lost control of some of the creatures, asked Aos for the ability to create additional powerful beings that could be assigned to control each group. Aos, understanding that these new gods would be much less powerful than himself, agreed to allow Melandor to continue.

Being closer to these beings than Aos, Melandor decided to exert his power over the creatures by creating new deities based on how the creatures prayed to him and other non-existing gods. He created:

  • Ortune, the goddess of law and justice
  • Gedis, the god of birth and renewal
  • and Nola, the goddess of fortune and luck.

For a time, this seemed to work to control the creatures and some level of order returned to the Eldarums. Pleased, he rested and watched to see how these new deities interacted with the beings and controlled their destinies.

Nila soon realized that a problem existed. When these creatures died, their existence was over. The idea that her elemental creations and perhaps even herself or Aos could have their existence ended forever didn’t agree with her. Based on the ever-evolving creatures she watched who had the idea that their life would continue after their death, she decided to do something about it. She did what she saw Aos and Melandor do in the past and used a piece of her own essence to create Famera, the Goddess of the underworld. She tasked Famera with retaining a portion of the creature’s essence even after death. To accomplish the request of her patron, the newly created Famera proceeded to create “The Underworld”. The Underworld was created to become a shadow of the life the creatures lived in.

Unbeknownst to Famera, the creation of the Underworld resulted in another plane. When Nila created Famera, she wanted to ensure that Nature was in balance. When Famera created the Underworld, this feature inadvertently created a place that was also a mirror dimension of the material world. But instead of death, this place teamed with life. The life here was different that the other life of the world. Somehow it had the ability to utilize the essence of the natural world to grant it unusual powers over nature. Nila, realizing this found it amusing and referred to it as the “Fey Realm.”

As life progressed, Melandor, watching his new creations, soon realized that the interests of the followers continued to diverge. In an effort to keep up with the demands of the various creatures, he created additional deities over these mortals. First, he created Halzatra to control some of the more basic instincts of the creatures. Shortly after he added to the dieties:

  • Umaldir, God of Birth and Renewal
  • Adalle, Goddess of Light
  • And Maesis, God of Protection.

Time continued to pass and before long, Melandor struggled to contain the desires of his creatures. In a final attempt to satiate them, he created the final deities over them:

  • Phenir, God of Courage & Endurance
  • Emphy, God of Joy & Artistic Expression
  • Xyris, God of War
  • And Halzartra, The God of the Lost

With these final creations, Melandor realized the power he utilized to create all of the deities made his power weaker. Melandor, thinking that he finally had created gods to control all aspects of the creatures in his charge, deemed his work complete and decreed that his work was complete.

Melandor, exhausted from his work rested and allowed his under gods to take control over the creatures in his charge. Over the course of millennium, he eventually realized that he had made a great error. In creating these gods in the image of the creatures they represent; he created them in forms of the creatures and in both male and female genders. Having spent the majority of his power creating them, he left them to determine the fate of the world.

Before long, these new deities gave birth to their own offspring, creating many new offspring. These new beings, being made of multiple gods of different creature types formed entirely new gods over the various creatures of the Eldarums. These new gods seemed incompatible to some extent to some of the existing gods and they grabbed what power they could over the beings of the world.

The chaos that ensued also offered opportunity. Halzartra, having been given control over those who do not follow the others, built a following. At this point the gods realized that the more followers they have, the more power they have over the world. Due to many creatures not acquiescing to the gods power, Halzartra become more powerful than the rest of the gods. The new offspring of the earlier gods, look to also gain power. Agreeing to follow Halzartra, he broke down his groups of followers and assigned them to the new gods.

Halzartra empowered the offspring of the gods to separate from their parents and have their own following. He decried the following gods and their domains:

  • Nheazen, God of Fallen Souls
  • Rathos, God of Death
  • Anos, Goddess of Tyranny
  • Qottar, God of Thievery
  • Formysus, Goddess of Disease & Misfortune
  • Atahn, God of Violence & Murder
  • Baduesus, God of Darkness & Shadow
  • and Phorena, Goddess of Lies & Deceit

These gods immediately took control over their domains and were determined to gain power. Drawing from the worst emotions of creatures, their power grew. At first, the original gods created by Melandor allowed these creatures to seek the will of these new deities. It didn’t take long before a power struggle grew amongst the various gods.

In an effort to gain more control, the original deities paired with some of the new gods. The offspring of these unions resulted in what would be the last of the gods: Dhotarr, Drynja, Luzueses, Ithar, Kyratrix, and Tysus. Despite the good intentions of the gods, the creation of these new dieties created even more chaos amongst the pantheon. With so many deities, each struggled to maintain control over their flock of creatures. These struggles poured over to their followers who in turn started to battle amongst each other. Wars broke out throughout the lands with many dying in the name of their god.

Aos and Nila who resided to let their creations take control of the creatures, decried the end to the creation of gods. With this decry, all formation of gods was ended and only Aos would be capable of creating new gods. Unfortunately, the damage was already done. Without reducing the number of gods, the gods that already existed continued to battle for supremacy over the creatures. This struggled continued for around 40,000 years.

As the gods struggled for power, the newest of them fought feverishly over control of all of the domains. In this war, the gods decry that they should be removed from power in what is called “The Purge”. These gods were captured, put in celestial bindings, and their power over the creatures of the world was blocked, effectively blocking their power for eternity. Halzartra and the other gods created during his time were seen as the perpetuator of the unrest and were relinquished to have control only those who followed evil tendencies. He was referred to as “The Fallen One”. The Gods had used much of their power to affect this binding and banishment, but never-the-less, a small group of creatures still maintained their allegiance to them. Various historical writings offer insight into “The Purge” and the continuation of this following and date this event to 6,000 PB – 3,000 PB.

After this struggle, peace fell over the various Eldarums. The extended peace allowed for the humanoid races to put group together to once and for all form group with the same beliefs. The first emergency of tribes began around 2,500 PB. During this time, the various creatures formed like-minded groups and the various races started to become one. Dragons were the first creature to join and then quickly separate as the realized the vast differences in their beliefs. In particular, the Metallic dragons quickly formed a distaste to the Chromatics and Gem Dragons decided to separate from both and live amongst the wild.

As tribes of different creatures emerged, they lived in harmony amongst each other even though some of them tended towards an evil nature. The various creature types would at times battle each other, but generally the peace was kept. As the time passed and the ability to travel increased with technology, the overall population increased, and the various races formed factions. Some of these creature factions began to align with Halzatra, forming the “Evil Races”.

As the power of Halzatra and the power of Evil spread, the remaining gods of good set a charge on their followers to seek out Evil and destroy it. The various good aligned creatures outweighed the evil creatures and this to the eventual rise and fall of Halzatra. With the prevalence of evil seemingly extinguished from the world, peace once again came to Eldarum. With a lasting peace, the various races flourished. These groups of people gathered and began to form Kingdoms. In a place known as the Zrelian Dynasty, the peoples created the first truly organized advanced culture in Eldarum. Loosing connection with the gods, these peoples saw their leader as their god incarnate. As this belief spread throughout the land, the Elven, Dwarven, Gnomish, Halfling, and Dragonborn peoples began to separate from the human brethren and formed their own religions based on the existing gods.

Ortune, the first of the offspring of Melandor realized the number of followers he would be granted by this union of God and human and granted the first Pharaoh some of his powers as a mortal. Through these powers, the Pharoah was granted renowned throughout the dynasty. This continued throughout the centuries as various Pharaohs were born and died. Eventually this power resulted in a larger following that the dynasty began to expand beyond their lands. This expansion caused turmoil to the lands of occupation and the telltale signs of war began to surface once again.

People in war are people in desperation, and desperation draws upon the worst in people. In this desperation, people called on an old god to control their destiny. Their plight fell to Dhotarr, The Crimson King, Betrayer God of Blood. This newly-found following of Dhotarr caused religions to form based on his power of the domain of war. Dhotarr, compelled by his followers broke his celestial bindings and again took the power granted to him as a god. The gods under Melandor believed this to be impossible and concerns grew over returning to a time of chaos and competition for power. This would not stand.

Dhotarr, with growing power, flexed his power against the other gods, threatening to release the other purged gods under his power. Not willing to allow the times of the past to reoccur, they vowed to stop his power. Dhotarr, responded by taking a page out of the book of Ortune. He took one of his most devout followers and imbued him with a midiron of his power. Pulling a piece of his body, he sent it to the material plane, transforming his follower into a gigantic nightmarish being of power.

As the godly power of Dhotarr was release on the world, his destruction was absolute. Serving the will of Dhotarr and his followers, it pushed back the Zrelian Dynasty’s gains and began to overwhelm the other races of the world. Ortune, not to be overpowered, selected his Pharoah god as his follower and sacrificed some of his own power to create a gigantic avatar of his own to battle Dhotarr’s Titan. With two Titans on the world, the destruction of both people and nature was immense.

The creation of these Titans, increased the followers of Ortune and Dhotarr. This increased following by the display of power drew attention and followers from the other gods. As they felt a reduction in power, they realized that they too needed to exert their influence over the beings of the world. In a waterfall effect, each utilized one of their followers to create Titan avatars in their image on the world. Together, these Titans wreaked havoc on the world and the destruction was immense. Nila, who was happily resting and watching the world that she helped create finally saw the immense destruction taking place on Eldarum. This destruction began to tear apart the whole cosmos as the gods battled. She determined that only the power of Nature could overcome these “Minor” gods. She demanded that Rasdon, Amino, Edtris, Otila, Toros, and Famera release their own Titans to combat those of the other gods. She demanded that Nature wipe out all who stands against its will.

The Natural gods acquest to Nila’s decree and through their own power created Titans based purely on their elemental nature. Utilizing the extensive power of the elements, these new Titans quickly started to destroy the other titans. The destruction was so immense, that the denizens of Eldarum realized the impending doom and took what cover they could. As the Nature Titans defeated their foes, the landscape of the world changed forever. Upon Nila’s decree and with the defeat of the final Titan, the Natural Titans sank into the landscape as a permanent protection against a further uprising. The Pantheon once again jointly imposed their will upon Dhotarr and bound him for eternity.

With the immense dead following the Titan Wars, the power of Famera grew. Her overwhelming power granted by the souls she retained was realized. Nila, having gained victory over the other gods was forced to abandon Famera and relinquish her banishment with the other bound gods. Given her control over the domain of shadow, she may prove too powerful for even the natural titans to regain power over the world.

With the end of the Titan wars, the world once again was in peace. This peace grew prosperity and the various creatures of the world once again grew in numbers. The balance of power within the realm of the gods was maintained and a peace once again fell upon the lands of Eldarum.
