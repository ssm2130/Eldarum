Overgod & nature sovereign

Aos is the god of gods: first intelligence, constructor of matter, creator of the pantheon. Most mortals never perceive him; those who try to worship him gain no structured spellcasting from that choice. Only Nila’s bond appears to bend his attention.

Nila, “Mother of Nature,” commands the elemental courts and proved decisive in the Titan Wars when Nature’s Titans unmade lesser god-forged avatars. The Fey Realm arose accidentally as mirror-vitality when the Underworld was founded—Nila named the paradox and left it to grow.

Dhotarr — Soul Crusher (expanded)

Betrayer gods were historically censored by epithet so their true names might starve for worship. In polite society Dhotarr is “The Soul Crusher”: god of oppression, blood, and war whose will twists even nature to submission. After failing to seize the Nine Hells during The Purge, celestial bindings trapped him—yet cults across Eldarum still whisper his titles.

During the Zrelian crisis, desperation restored his cult; Dhotarr shattered his bindings, blessed a follower with Titan-scale power, and triggered the spiral of god-forged avatars that became the Titan Wars. Nature’s retaliation under Nila put the world back in balance and rebound Dhotarr—but at the cost of empowering Famera’s soul-empire.

Appearance. Iron-clad warrior wreathed in shadow; hollow helm; colossal obsidian warhammer likewise veiled. His battlefield presence cowed even other gods.

Worship. The Avatars of Torment venerate him; brutal humanoid tribes and pit-fighters sometimes scar his glyph into flesh. Tie to the apocryphal Crushing Depths beneath Asceveron appears in Religion-lore and Inye’s notes.

Relationships. He conspired with Baduesus before the Purge; shadow clings to him still.

Ithar — Annihilator (Eldarum canon)

Ithar is not a conqueror-god but a paraclete of undone outcomes: the seductive proof that nothing built deserves to endure. Where kin betrayers hunger for crowns, Ithar’s liturgy arrives as migraines of geometry — boulevards confessing they were always rubble, treaties erasing themselves mid-signature, rivers unpicking their banks from shame.

Titan imagery attached to Ithar skews toward continent-scale vermiform silhouettes — tunneling without soil, leaving logic porous. The Purge bound Ithar with celestial shackles alongside other betrayers, yet philosophy departments, dying empires, and revisionist printers still spread Ithar’s equations under euphemism.

At table: use Knowledge and Trickery domains as vectors for idea-assassination — stolen memories sold back as revelations, maps that omit tomorrow.
