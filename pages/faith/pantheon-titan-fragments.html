The Titans of Eldarum

Collected fragments from pre-Bane archives, temple records, and forbidden accounts — parallel witness to Titanic Records, with tighter vignettes on named Titans.

Preface: On the Nature of Titans

“They were not born. They were not summoned. They were imposed.” — Arch-Scholar Velm Iridan, On Divine Catastrophe

The beings now called Titans were not known by that name during the age of their existence. Contemporary accounts refer to them as Manifest Judgments, Divine Instruments, Walking Ends. Only centuries later did survivors give them a simpler name — Titans — not out of accuracy, but necessity. Language failed in their presence.

The Celestial Alignment and First Manifestation

During the Celestial Alignment, when the suns Vils and Vols merged their light into a green radiance and the moons aligned in silent convergence, the boundary between realms thinned to near-nothing.

Witnesses describe three sequential phenomena: Silence of Wind (even storms ceased); Color Drain (the world dimmed briefly); The Green Flood (light returned, unnatural and total). It was in this final moment that the first Titan appeared — not summoned, not arriving — simply present.

On Their Form and Scale

No two accounts agree entirely, but all confirm: Titans did not occupy space—they dominated it; distance distorted around them; scale shifted with perspective. It stood beyond the mountains, yet its shadow fell upon us as if it were beside us.

Scholars theorize Titans existed partially outside the Material Plane, anchoring only enough of themselves to act.

The Titan Wars (Commonly Misunderstood)

Contrary to popular retellings, the Titan Wars were not battles between Titans alone. They were competing expressions of divine intent imposed simultaneously upon a world unable to sustain them — yielding overlapping realities, contradictory physics, regions where time moved differently within the same battlefield.

The Scourge (Eyewitness Fragments)

“The sky split not once, but continuously.” “The ground did not break—it surrendered.” “The air burned though there was no flame.”

The Scourge marked the moment when Nila’s intervention forced systemic collapse: all Titans destabilized at once — chain reactions of divine energy, detonations beyond mortal comprehension, fragmentation of Titan essence into Shards.

Titan Remains: The Living World

Much of Eldarum’s geography is not natural — it is anatomical. Disputed texts cite a mountain ridge as Terravorex’s spinal column; a dusk-pulsing valley as collapsed circulation; glass spiral deserts as residual heat from Pyrathos.

Accounts of Specific Titans

Pyrathos, The World Pyre. We saw the horizon ignite before we saw him. Villages reported fire spreading against the wind, rivers boiling from underground, shadows burning into walls; after its fall the land stayed warm for three generations.

Fulgaroth, The Storm Tyrant. It did not walk. It was the storm. Lightning preceded thunder’s existence; armies vanished in white light; metal liquefied mid-battle. Some texts claim Fulgaroth persists inside storms.

Seraphiel, Light Incarnate. We thought it salvation. We were wrong. Blinding brilliance; healing became cancerous overgrowth; purification erased identity.

Malpherion, The Fallen Host. It wept ash. Broken wings; shadows lengthened permanently; the dead neither rose nor rested.

Vorulix, The Void Harvester. The town folded inward. Wind pulled downward; structures imploded; sound ceased before destruction.

The Shards (Relics of Cataclysm)

After Titans fell, fragments remained — not debris but essence. Shards carry residual will, domain-flavored effects, resistance to mundane destruction. A shard does not grant power. It replaces something with power.

The Magi-Bane (The Silence of Power)

In the centuries following the war: magic ceased; rituals failed; divine connection weakened. Theories include Arcane Saturation Collapse, Divine Withdrawal Pact, Shard Interference Field — no consensus.

The Underworld Connection

Increasingly accepted: the Underworld formed where Titan death-energy exceeded stability. Severe battle sites show planar instability and mirrored terrain; Underworld skies echo the Alignment’s green.

Famera’s Role (Reinterpreted)

Tradition paints opportunism; newer scholarship argues Famera (Ala) attempted to bottle excess death-energy — making later punishment political as much as moral. Highly disputed.

Modern Implications

Some Titans may not be fully dead; shards keep surfacing; the Underworld expands subtly; records warn another Celestial Alignment is possible.

Closing Excerpt

“We tell ourselves the Titans are gone because we require that to be true.” “But the world still bears their shape.” — Last entry of Archivist Senrail (disappeared)
