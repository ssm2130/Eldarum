I. Preface: On Truth and Memory

“History is not what happened. It is what survived.”

This manuscript is not a single account.

It is a compilation of fragments, often contradictory, occasionally heretical, and frequently incomplete. Many primary sources were:

  • Burned during the aftermath of the Titan Wars
  • Sealed by religious authorities
  • Lost during the Magi-Bane

What remains is assembled here not as certainty—but as evidence.

II. The Celestial Alignment

The event now known as the Celestial Alignment is universally agreed to have preceded the Titan Wars, though its exact frequency remains unknown.

Observed phenomena. Recovered records consistently describe:

  • The merging light of Vils (yellow) and Vols (blue) into a green radiance
  • Simultaneous visibility of all three moons:
    • Raral (dreaming blue)
    • Tivoli (white solace)
    • Honus (burning amber)

Phase sequence.

  • Atmospheric stillness
  • Dimming of natural light
  • Sudden total illumination (green spectrum)
  • Auditory nullification (reported “world silence”)

Scholarly consensus. The Alignment caused:

  • Planar permeability
  • Leakage of outer planar energies:
    • Aenimar (positive)
    • Yasslyn (neutral knowledge)
    • Iocetis (law)
    • Uccasos, Dritora, Zaezirus (negative forces)

III. The Manifestation of Titans

“They did not arrive. They were suddenly unavoidable.”

The beings later called Titans appeared during peak alignment.

Key observations

  • No summoning rituals recorded
  • No portals observed
  • No descent from sky or emergence from ground

Instead: They simply existed where they had not existed before.

Theoretical model: partial manifestation. Modern arcane theory suggests Titans were divine projections anchored partially in the Material Plane and sustained by planar overlap. This explains shifting scale, resistance to physical laws, and catastrophic environmental impact.

IV. The Titan Wars

The Titan Wars were not traditional warfare. They were simultaneous impositions of incompatible divine realities.

Battlefield conditions. Records indicate:

  • Regions where gravity reversed intermittently
  • Areas where time repeated in loops
  • Landscapes transforming mid-conflict

“We fled across the same hill three times before realizing it was not the same hill.”

Mortals in the war. Mortals were not participants—they were observers, victims, occasionally collateral tools. Entire civilizations vanished without structural damage, collapsed from within, or were reshaped into unrecognizable forms.

V. The Scourge and Collapse

Nila’s intervention. The Goddess of Nature deployed multiple Titan-level manifestations simultaneously. Their purpose: not victory—termination.

The cascade event. All Titans destabilized at once. Effects included massive energy discharges, planar rupture, and divine fragmentation. This moment is now called the Scourge.

Aftermath. Titans ceased coordinated activity; many collapsed or disintegrated; the world was left structurally altered.

VI. The Remains of Titans

“The land remembers what we do not.”

Eldarum’s geography is not entirely natural.

Types of remains

  • Anatomical landscapes: mountain ranges as skeletal structures; valleys as impact depressions; forest anomalies as residual life-energy zones.
  • Dormant masses: some Titans did not die—they became inert, entered stasis, remain buried beneath the surface.
  • Living sites: pulsation, reactive terrain, magical instability — believed partially active Titan remnants.

VII. Recorded Titan Accounts

(Compiled from eyewitness testimony and secondary reconstruction.)

Pyrathos, The World Pyre. Fire spread against environmental logic; heat persisted for generations; stone liquefied at distance. We saw the future of the world—and it was ash.

Terravorex, The World Devourer. Entire cities swallowed intact; terrain absorbed into its body; sinkholes formed in its wake.

Fulgaroth, The Storm Tyrant. Lightning preceded sound; entire armies erased instantly; storms remained active years after disappearance.

Seraphiel, Light Incarnate. Healing caused uncontrolled biological growth; light erased shadows permanently; survivors reported loss of identity.

Vorulix, The Void Harvester. Structures collapsed inward; air movement reversed direction; survivors extremely rare.

Malpherion, The Fallen Host. Ashfall followed its movement; shadows behaved independently; corpses resisted decay but did not animate.

VIII. The Shards of Divinity

Following the collapse, fragments remained.

Nature of shards: compressed divine essence; domain-specific influence; resist destruction by conventional means.

Observed effects: enhancement of mortal ability; gradual personality alteration; environmental distortion. It does not empower you. It replaces what you were.

Classification: minor (limited, short-term); major (significant, persistent); prime (reality-warping, often guarded or lost).

IX. The Magi-Bane

Following the Titan Wars: magic ceased across Eldarum; divine communication diminished; arcane knowledge became inert.

Leading theories: Arcane Saturation Collapse; Divine Withdrawal Agreement; Shard Interference Field.

Duration: unknown — records vary from decades to centuries.

X. The Underworld Hypothesis

A controversial but increasingly supported theory: the Underworld formed as a byproduct of Titan death.

Supporting evidence: locations of intense Titan conflict show planar instability and mirrored environments; the Underworld sky shares the green hue of the Alignment.

Conclusion: the Underworld is likely a decayed echo of the Material Plane, formed where reality failed to stabilize.

XI. Religious Interpretations

  • Followers of Melandor: Titans were necessary corrections; the war was tragic but required.
  • Followers of Halzatra: Titans proved divine supremacy; destruction is the natural state.
  • Druids of Nila: Titans were imbalances corrected; Nature restored what gods disrupted.
  • Underworld cults (Famera): Titans were transitional beings; decay is the final truth of existence.

XII. Contradictions in Record

Not all accounts align. Examples: some claim Titans fought each other; others claim they ignored each other entirely. Some say they were controlled; others insist independence.

Conclusion: There is no single correct version of events — only overlapping truths.

XIII. Modern Implications

Though centuries have passed: shards continue to surface; regions remain unstable; the Underworld encroaches slowly.

Most concerning finding: recent celestial observations suggest another Alignment is possible.

XIV. Final Warnings

“We have mistaken silence for safety.” “The Titans did not leave. They stopped.”

— Recovered inscription, origin unknown
