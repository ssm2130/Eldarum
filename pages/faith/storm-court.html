Storm Court heralds

Liturgy as verdict — bells, salt, and windward confession.

The Storm Court does not worship weather; it worships jurisdiction expressed as weather. Coastal chapels hang bells beside tide clocks so noon means something different when the Greywake decides to argue with the moon. Priests train voices to carry across surf—the Storm Lord hears sincerity better when confession has to compete with spray.

Rites that scale with wind

Confession faces seaward so privacy scales with gusts: whisper on calm days when sins are bookkeeping; shout into gales when guilt needs the sky as witness. Salt crosses foreheads in patterns borrowed from admiralty law—each grain a miniature boundary stone between mercy and reprisal. Visitors mistake the theatrics for maritime superstition until they watch a herald bind a treaty while lightning writes marginalia across the clouds.

Beliefs in brief

Storm adherents claim justice without storm is merely paperwork; mercy without shelter is theater. Pilgrims carry copper pins bent into jagged lines to signify interrupted pride—the same pins coastal towns leave at shrines when pride costs someone a fleet.

Political role

Stammont crowns historically patronize Storm chapels along invasion-prone shores; donations spike after Zrelian raids. Reformers whisper the Storm Court’s independence threatens secular law—traditionalists reply secular law never stopped tide.

Adventurer hooks

Competing temples sometimes weaponize omens—verify which bell toll is theater and which is warning before you march on someone’s tithe. Recover relic bells stolen to mute coastal watches, because silence here buys shipwrecks wholesale. When ash seasons clog Zehas shrines, Storm priests and volcano clergy negotiate truces loud enough for harbor crowds—mediating that noise pays in royal letters and forgiven harbor fees.

Titan Wars afterimage

Storm networks once synchronized divine interventions across fronts like artillery clocks; modern auguries may be residue from that artillery liturgy—bells answering bells across harbors that no longer share a crown. Read the integrated chronicle in Titan Wars before treating any coastal miracle as purely local. Canonical theology remains under the Pantheon archive.
