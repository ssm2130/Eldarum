What is a genius locus?

A genius locus is not a tame god—it is geography with opinions. Titans left them like unpaid interns: tending passes, remembering kilns, sulking in tide pools until mortals bring offerings coded as apologies. Some loci negotiate with multiple temples; others refuse all scripture until someone repairs the road they liked.

Temperaments & rituals

Scholars sort loci by temperament: guardian (obsessive about borders), forge (obsessive about transformation), weeping (obsessive about debts unpaid). Rituals vary—some regions leave offerings of ink, iron filings, or staged apologies performed by actors trained to cry convincingly.

Titan remains & patrons

Expeditions hunt ossified strata rumored in volcanic uplands—bones mistaken for ore, nerves mistaken for gemstone seams. Patrons such as Kul Buldar finance probes balancing archaeology against accidental excavation of opinions better left buried.

Neutral loci & divine attention

Neutral Titans sound like oxymorons until diplomats realize neutrality scales with catastrophe. Rumors claim some loci broker between jealous gods; others feast on divine attention like barnacles on hulls—either way, mortals pay dock fees in blood and verse.
