Elliswich Quag

Sunken battle-mud where Godswar weather still argues with the map—Stammont’s northern gate to Greenveil and every secret the fen can keep.

Broadsheets call it the Elliswich Quag; older marsh guides say the Mire of Albermer—a quilt of peat and sucking channels spreading roughly thirty miles north of Towfort across ground that remembers divine warfare as chemistry: purple stems, wrong tides in still pools, and briars that open only when moons agree. Scholars tie the bog to Titan-era clashes mirrored on shard lore; smugglers tie it to profit.

Towfort and the march north

Crown roads fade into plank walks and pole-boats; Collacht magistrates at Towfort pretend jurisdiction ends where chalk does. Pilgrims and shard-hunters share ferries with ceramic merchants whose manifests smell like honest glaze and read like coded poetry—see campaign handouts for Shepherd fronts moving goods east from the swamp. Anything requiring signatures, scaffold rhetoric, or Finnius-shaped favors stages through Towfort before the mud takes the narrative.

Beggar’s Hole

Deep inside the maze, Beggar’s Hole sits as a hidden freehold—thorn veils, reed-board ambushes, and elders who treat cartography as treason. Storm League contact Brothi (lizardfolk) met Amara Stormborn here with warnings about solo travel and a pointer toward adventurers already headed toward Greenvale. Session-grade hospitality tests, memory geasa, and kin welcomes that sideline humans belong to table prose on the Campaign journal — Beggar’s Hole.

Dearil’s Keep and the astral hinge

Gatehouse politics mesh with predator patience: bullywugs, ogre tolls, troll bridges, and rumor of hoard drakes lounging on flooded vaults. Fiadh’s threads tie to kin and pursuit through Dearil’s cruelty—beat-for-beat escalation lives in journal — Elliswich & Dearil’s Keep. Farther out, Greenveil astral ruins reward lunar riddles when parties trade swamp grit for constellation keys.

Shards and philosophy

Inye reached this fen after Lord Alan Neylan named it from Dukbini archives—the largest Godswar residue on Asceveron’s northern isle, swallowing ancient battlefields until marsh swallowed them back. Her patron’s pilgrimage overlaps Amara’s Storm League circuit where thunder and theology share the same storm front.
