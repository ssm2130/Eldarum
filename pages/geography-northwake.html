Geography of the Northwake

Mountains, rivers, and the winds that decide battles.

The Northwake is not a kingdom but a weather pattern with politics inside it. Bounded by the Highfold heights to the south and the Greywake chopping seas to the north, the region stitches Stammont’s productive heartland to ports that stare toward Zrelian sails.

Rivers and roads

The Three Sisters rivers—Velen, Ostro, and Mir—descend from snowfields to Collimar’s basin. Velen carries wheat; Ostro carries timber; Mir carries gossip. Royal engineers maintain cobbled highways along Velen’s banks; Ostro’s logging roads belong to axe-bitumen and seasonal mud. Pilgrims claim Mir’s fog hides omens; toll collectors claim it hides smugglers. Both are correct.

Forests and borders

North of Towfort, the Timberwild thickens into root-maze glades where crown surveyors disappear and rangers return with contradictory maps. Orcish banners—especially the Black Tusk coalition—exploit these gaps between parchment and soil. Eastern approaches toward Greiser favor trade caravans but suffer spring floods.

Climate bands

Coastal Darkshore trades salt spray for caution: storms ride in fast, bell towers double as foghorns, and soldiers drill in slick cobbles. Minwall’s northern shore receives pale summers and brittle winters; ice sometimes bridges minor inlets before cracking with sounds like cathedral glass. Volcanic highlands near Zehas breathe warm downslope winds that confuse vintners and bless certain orchards twice a year.

Natural hazards

  • Ash tides: Zehas outflows create vivid sunsets and respiratory ailments.
  • Razor tides: Certain Greywake currents align to produce standing waves lethal to small craft.
  • Old roads: Pre-imperial paving occasionally hums under wagons—genius loci echoes, claim scholars.

Political geography—who taxes the fog

Stammont’s crown lattice reaches downriver along Velen barges until frontier charters blunt it—Asceveron march towns buy autonomy with levies and couriers. Zrelian admiralties pressure sea approaches; inland, Greiser syndicates monetize gates; offshore, Kazon auctioneers monetize deniability. No border holds perfectly; every hedge is a negotiation with weather.
