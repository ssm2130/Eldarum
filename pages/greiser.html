Greiser

Market town at a crossroads of pilgrimage and profit.

Greiser flourishes where eastern grain routes kiss pilgrim trails bound for storm-shrines on the coast. Inns stack travelers three-deep during harvest; abbots negotiate bulk blessings with the same ledgers merchants use for barley futures.

Culture

Street theaters satirize kings safely by replacing crowns with cooking pots. A famous bridge bears graffiti from three centuries of failed revolutions—preserved as “heritage vandalism” by civic decree.

Tolls, saints, and statute books

Greiser’s gates earn more poetry than wars: guild syndics measure pilgrim traffic against grain futures and publish overlapping schedules—some keyed to AB fiscal years, others keyed to saints’ comets nobody alive has witnessed. Judges import Compact clauses when crowns argue; mercenaries import excuses when crowns blink.

Road lore vs canon pages

Everything travelers hear in Greiser taprooms should entertain first—then defer to Master timeline synthesis and primary sources when rebuilding articles for consistency.
