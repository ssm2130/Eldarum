Chronicle of Ages

Broad strokes historians pretend they agree upon.

Eldarum’s histories fracture along sponsor lines—Stammont annals emphasize legitimate succession; Zrelian cyclicals emphasize renewal through flame; monastery codices emphasize whichever donor paid last month’s ink. Below is a pragmatic synthesis players may encounter in universities, barracks, and taverns priced for mercenaries.

Eras at a glance

  1. Mythic Prelude: Titans shape continents; genius loci crystallize when divine attention slackens (Age of Titans). PB reckoning dominates temple copies.
  2. Purge Mythography: Betrayer gods bound; censored epithets multiply; evil learns to incorporate.
  3. Blood Meridian Wars: Mortal kings attempt land bridges between incompatible magics—ruins proliferate faster than masons.
  4. Zrelian Dynasty strain: Pharaoh-theology turns expansion into liturgy until Titan proxies answer insult with continent-scale punctuation.
  5. Titan Wars & Shattering: Prime avatars clash; Echo Titans linger as unstable wardens; shard hills rewrite prospecting (modern chronicle page).
  6. Arcane Convalescence: Libraries grey; rituals fail politely; genius loci inherit unpaid chores.
  7. Magi-Bane memory: Gods throttle dangerous grammars; some magical schools vanish from pedagogy for generations.
  8. Ash Compact Period: Crowns negotiate with volcanoes via intermediaries priests still argue about.
  9. Rise of Harbor Law: Admiralties codify bell signals; piracy becomes taxable enterprise in places like Kazon.
  10. Three-Crown Compact: Formalizes succession arbitration across Northwake realms (details).
  11. Present Tempest: Naval rivalry with Zrelia, frontier cult activity, rumors of divine mobilization (Recent Strife).

Northwake through calendar smoke

Stammont’s annals prefer parchment dignity: successions measured in legitimate births and legitimate sieges. Zrelian cyclicals prefer fire: admiralties renew themselves by staging controlled catastrophes at sea—exercises misread as prophecy by merchants who insure hulls against theology. Between them lies the Greywake, where neutral water pretends not to favor hull paint.

Frontier chronicles—Towfort ballads, Baycliff watcher logs, Elliswich fen guides—preserve dates scribes omit. Treat them as ethnography: they explain why bread riots rhyme with tide tables.

Blood, bread, and theology

Major wars acquire sponsors posthumously. Black Tusk campaigns borrow rhetoric from Titan strata; Storm Court sermons borrow thunder from budgets; cultists borrow legitimacy from whichever crown blinked last. When peasants say “the age turned,” they often mean tax assessors learned new arithmetic.

Historiography

“Truth” in Eldarum is often an average of fears. Titan bones beneath mountains become metaphors for mining rights; eclipse chronologies become propaganda for tax holidays. Adventurers entering archives should pack patience, garlic for ink-spites, and coin for archivists who rent keys by the hour.
