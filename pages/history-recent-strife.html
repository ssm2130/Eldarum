Recent Strife

Why supply wagons burn and bells wear out faster.

Court calendars in the mid–1500s AB register a sour decade: Zehas exhaling unpredictably, Zrelian fleets testing Darkshore nerves, corsairs rebranding as privateers whenever treaties blink. Beneath geopolitics runs theological rumor—gods aligning like fleets, temples hoarding miracles for bargaining chips, relic-lawyers arguing whether shard possession voids citizenship.

Military friction

Supply columns suffer orchestrated raids mirroring Towfort tactics rehearsed against orcish pressures—some blame Black Tusk survivors staging graduation exercises without permits, others whisper cultists infiltrating baggage trains disguised as quartermasters’ cousins.

Stammont’s admiralty presses ballista chain-shot and siege engines toward every coastal crown city; guild factors hire adventurers as brokers just to pretend neutrality. Zrelian hulls rehearse narrows timings until lantern grammar mistakes read as declarations—mistakes paid in splinters.

Occult friction

Storm auguries multiply; priests argue whether lightning frequency signals divine mobilization or Titan dreams surfacing through Zehas strata. Neutral Titan lore—if “neutral” exists—alarms monastic cartographers who measure morality in watersheds and publish maps politicians burn.

Frontier covens trade in relic whispers: shards that hunger, swords that remember astral dreadnoughts, decks of cards that rewrite patience. Collimar’s archives charge hazard rates for anyone requesting “succession omens” during the same week as a Compact examination—price doubles if you smell like seawater.

Economic smoke signals

Grain barge schedules slip when Mirror Reach pilots report silver wakes—either honest tariffs or smugglers’ mirrors. Kazon auction houses laugh at customs forms; Stammont customs officials laugh back with ledgers; everyone loses except poem-writers.

Civilian costs

Bread riots rarely topple crowns but frequently hire mercenaries who later become tax collectors—history’s favorite promotion. Frontier towns stockpile grain beneath hymn-singing volunteers paid in charm-beads and legend.

Ash seasons rename children after winds; Elliswich fen families sweep roofs together when Zehas drifts east—symbolic forgiveness borrowed from volcanic etiquette. Refugees compare notes on which crowns honor Compact debt holidays and which crowns merely delay interest with prettier flags.

What scholars agree on

Almost nothing—except that the Greywake has never been neutral, only patient. For the long arc from Titan prelude to this century, read Master timeline; for regional anchors, Asceveron march and Northwake geography.
