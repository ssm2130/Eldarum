Three-Crown Compact

Northwake treaty law that makes successions expensive but survivable.

Signed after a century of disputed regencies nearly let Zrelian admirals rewrite Stammont estuary maps by fire, the Three-Crown Compact bound major Northwake houses to arbitration by a triad of crowns—earth, sea, and storm—represented through relic regalia kept in Collimar’s cipher vaults.

Key clauses (simplified)

Succession: Disputed heirs submit to examination by clerics of the Veiled Pantheon and admiralty scribes—tests vary between theological essays and naval knot exams depending on bribery calendars.

Tri-crown arbitration: Earth regalia (vaulted soils sample), sea regalia (admiralty log sealed in brine glass), and storm regalia (tempest ribbon struck thrice in Collimar’s cipher courts) must concur—or disagree in writing—to certify a monarch.

Frontier charter: Regions like Asceveron may negotiate semi-autonomous tariffs if they supply crowns with agreed-upon levies and emergency couriers.

Harbor reciprocity: Zrelian admiralties gain right of neutral anchorage in designated Mirror Reach bights during succession freezes; Stammont batteries hold fire except when bell codes declare “practice” or “theater.”

Emergency doctrine: Storm omens verified by three independent temples suspend debt collections—historically abused once per generation.

Archival poison pill: Tampering with Collimar’s relic vaults voids the compact for the offending house until public penance financed by said house—lawyers argue whether penance includes sponsoring a cathedral or merely a festival.

Fictional precision—why dates disagree

Signing ceremonies cluster in early AB centuries depending which archivist you bribe. Stammont favors winter coronations to excuse mud on rival banners; Zrelia prefers equinox fires so smoke photographs well for propaganda paintings. Treat every “year of signing” as a performance; the clauses matter more than the curtain call.

Enforcement on the ground

Towfort captains learn Compact procedure like drill: when succession stalls, mercenary contracts flip from crown-colored to charter-neutral within hours. Smugglers along the Greiser road celebrate “grey weeks” when arbitration delays inspections—same weeks grain inspectors invoke emergency exemptions. Everyone wins except whoever scheduled honesty.

Lawyers treasure loopholes; peasants treasure holidays triggered by procedural delays; adventurers treasure confusion lucrative to everyone except taxpayers.
