Sources & dating

This chronicle weaves GM planning notes into one narrative spine. Where drafts contradicted each other, priority goes to events echoed on the Campaign journal and Roll20 handouts.

Court calendars in play skew to 1558–1559 AB—the same window as broadsheet years elsewhere on this wiki. Where the manuscript table lists dual “pre-reckoning” labels beside AB-style counts, this article uses AB for mortal politics and PB only for divine prehistory, matching Master timeline notation.

Deep time — myth to Magi-Bane

In the scholarly shorthand preserved in royal tutors’ crib sheets, creation’s abundance yields first to elemental breath—fire, sea, and stone waking before wind and storm lent weather its opinions. Good powers rose; savage ages bred first mortal populations; jealousies among the righteous seeded the fallen courts later codified as betrayer theology. The Purge centuries bracket divine judgment so harsh that evil became something jurisdictions could indict instead of merely endure.

Mortal centuries rolled forward: Zrhalian speech and dynasty ambition climbed from river mud; pharaoh-kings styled themselves continuity with heaven; off-continent wars taught empires to spend fleets like vowels. When divine proxies exhausted patience, the Titan Wars stacked Prime against Echo until nature’s answer—the Scourge host—ended open continent-breaking battle. Zrelia’s magisters eventually abandoned northern holdings rather than hold ground against exhaustion and shard-sick soil.

Magi-Bane marks year 0 AB in this chronicle’s court math: a conscious severance when magic’s floodgates slammed shut, then reopened under oath-throttle so schools starved for generations. Stammont’s crown coalesces near the end of that drought (89 AB); the Sverge Republic charters merchant logic into governance (212 AB). By 689 AB archons declare Magi-Bane “lifted” for practical pedagogy—meaning controlled, taxed, and jealous—while Zrelia’s dynasty rebuilds spiritual continuity (922 AB).

Northwake crowns & long wars

From 1105 AB, Zrelian doctrine again tests Stammont marches—first raids, then bastion lines. Two centuries of mutual exhaustion (1142–1354 AB) burn Greywake budgets until a brittle peace (1355 AB). Espionage becomes trade policy (1367 AB); a tunnelled invasion fails (1373 AB); admiralties trade hull paint for sea rights (1402–1547 AB). The Sailors’ Tomb clash (1550 AB) leaves both crowns sick of funeral oil; 1551 AB armistice ink buys a decade of insults disguised as diplomacy—long enough for colonels to forget why they hated each other but not long enough for harbors to forgive.

Prelude — 1550s tensions

By 1558 AB broadsheets speak of rising tension again: Storm auguries, Zehas smoke, Greywake captains betting on which crown blinks first. Into that pressure arrives an adventuring ring—foreign specialists, crown-adjacent hopefuls, and divine creditors—whose convergence stories scatter across ports from Baycliff to Narlow.

Assembly & Leister debut

The party forms on Asceveron soil; individual roads matter less than shared arrival in Roafort, jewel of Leister where the royal orbit still pretends the hinterland is polite. Patron threads—Kul Buldar’s ledger, Storm League errands, Vanguard warrants, Hazoret handlers, Obsidian vault maps—begin crossing in tavern smoke.

Bride, court, Red Wedding & plague

Lady Allie Craxton vanishes ahead of a arranged match; the rescue leads to cavern laboratories where Horrus Dune trades ethics for alchemy. He escapes by contingency magic; Allie returns under scrutiny. Admiral Craxton’s villa hosts a ball that maps Leister rumor economy—affairs, serial blades, Veil resistance, portal ink smuggled through respectable shops.

The public wedding becomes battlefield: Allie convulses into Quellion’s working; demon blisters spray contagion through clergy and kin. Gentle Repose buys hours; arcana proves the affliction layered (transmutation, abjuration, necromancy). The Obsidian Order’s archives and Reece O’Hannon’s patience steer investigators toward rare fungi and underdark commerce—foreshadowing longer wars than one antidote can empty.

Zrelian assault & the cure march

While heroes chase short-stem staghorn caps toward the northwest ranges, the world reheats: Zrelia strikes Stammont on multiple fronts—Darkshore naval furors, Tomb Bay crowded with burning Figureheads, inland towns sacked. Scouts whisper that enemy hulls threaded the Tempest Fury in the Far Reach—impossible until it isn’t.

Roafort locks down; infected transform into monsters and meet pyres; quarantine cages nobles still wrestling Blister sorcery. In the mushroom caves a beholder rises from depths; Grax trades position to drag Mere clear. The same hours shake the continent—Mere’s dream of fire walking mountains precedes an earthquake whose signature matches distant caldera tantrums.

Toufort, Elliswich & Beggar’s Hole

On Nogule festival days the ring drinks Roafort’s hospitality, watches arena bloodsport (grudges seeded with gladiator names), then rides toward Towfort and the Elliswich Quag. Flavius earns Collacht jurisprudence the hard way; Finnius Flax trades slavers’ vectors for discretion. The swamp delivers Spirit Ruins where Hayliel keeps an Arcadian seal, Abrakary codices sleep under burial water, and Beggar’s Hole tests consent with fey hospitality—cards drawn, dreams poisoned with purpose. Fiadh’s hunt for mentor Darragh crosses Dearil cruelty; Lord/Lady Dearil gatehouse politics become rehearsal for worse Collimar theater.

Greenveil, Kul Buldar & underworlds

Greenveil ruins answer lunar riddles—constellation wheels, zodiac plates, banshee choirs—until an astral portal disgorges devourers and Sazi’s courier tale. The Infinitous Codex escalates stakes beyond regional war. Descent toward Kul Buldar threads dwarf seals, relic hunger, giant galleries, and another movement of Horrus cult geometry beneath volcanic ribs.

Shard peaks & Zehas

Glacier quests pit climbers against yeti druids, avalanches, and Zirrith’s ice cathedral—Mooncreeper shipments overlap Julian’s longer cure mathematics. Southward, Zehas vomits ash across Zrelian streets; firenewts, salamanders, and princes of flame barter passage until Imix’s avatar crowns a planar throat—closure demands dispels worthy of legend.

Pirates, Narlow & the southern sea

Bloodtooth corsairs stage Dragontooth theater; island tombs hide prisoners rescued alongside Remi Forlette—Anocheth’s heartbeat. The Pirouette crossing toward Flame Heart Citadel endures sea dragons and impossible storms that shear minutes from maps. Ashore, Narlow’s prosperity masks crime waves engineered by infernal bookkeeping—warehouses, casinos, temples of self-love—until Gilbert Genest’s postings invite professionals toward the Crushing Depths gate and Malvolia’s salon.

Lucani — Colossus & Boxguay

In Katileo, the Colossus of Akari punctures clouds—Etherium veins power artificer economies; gold wyrms broker shards heavier than conscience. Expeditions crawl the titan’s vault-foot anatomy: red wizards, giant forges, modron eyes, half-forgotten lift rituals—some chambers waking only when Zrelian gadgetry resonates with imprisoned souls.

Adla, choked by Boxguay blight, forces the party through silk labyrinths toward Eastra—Arasta herald of disease’s goddess—who bargains for audience with Inye’s patron-touched mind. Tracks diverge through Highrock libraries, Collimar archives, and storm-wracked communion nights where bronze wings remember Vanguard names.

Collimar reckoning (toward level 14)

In the capital’s arena, a gladiator billed as Korg Bloodfury proves to be Fiadh reshaped—muscle, trauma, and Daedril leash-magic forged into spectacle. Pursuit through rain-soaked avenues reveals an estate bristling with ballistae and basement vaults hoarding tribute golems. Fiadh breaks chains long enough to wear dragon flesh against her friends; only exhausted negotiation leaves her alive—holding property, dignity fractured, alliances reduced to neutrality purchased in blood and coin.

Road map beyond

Draft outlines continue toward continent-spanning reckonings—Dhotarr tribunals, Malvolia’s last ledger page, Hazoret installations, Katie’s purpose, Julian’s cure terminus, Void dragons and sleeping titans—but table fate decides which prophecies graduate from margin notes. Consult canon tiers alongside journal motivations when prepping sessions past fourteen.

Unplayed roadmap sketches stack horrors in ascending order: Dortar Eterna sermons swallowing southern Sverge crowds; Kethtek Marghull alchemy crossing Lucani reliefs; ancient chromatic duels (blue, red, void) rehearsed as crown theater; Famera and titans named as endgame antagonists for shard-bearing travelers; trade sabotage and Hazoret engineering meant to capstone Zrelian grids. None are canon until your table burns the calendar page.

Canon tier: Treat this chronicle as Tier 4–5 hybrid — faithful to campaign manuscripts and scene prose, subordinate to Pantheon cosmology if a metaphysical claim ever conflicts.
