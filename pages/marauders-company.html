The Marauders Company

Supply-line guardians turned legend after catastrophically brave campaigns.

Chartered to hunt brigands threatening royal commerce between interior cities, the Marauders Company earned praise for decisive strikes—and scrutiny after losses mounted near Towfort. Two hundred soldiers once marched under a captain famed for reading terrain like scripture; thirty-two returned from one raid against the Black Tusk leadership cluster—survivors’ testimonies disagree on whether victory or curse shaped the retreat, but every ledger agrees the company learned humility in arithmetic.

Doctrine

Night penetration, officer-led wedges, aggressive pursuit of command elements—tactics effective until shamans weaponize morale itself. Medical corps embedded curse-theorists alongside chirurgeons because some wounds persist in payroll; quartermasters learned to budget for hexed provisions spoiling impossibly fast while auditors demanded receipts written in ink that never dries on honest paper.

Legacy

Veterans carry mixed honors—medals minted thin as guilt. Command cadres rotate into advisory roles training next-generation escorts along Velen’s bargeways. Ballads omit paperwork; widows remember it verbatim. When crown histories need a cautionary tale about supply lines pretending geography is harmless, lecturers still invoke the Marauders’ Towfort season.
