Tier 1 — Pantheon archive & Titanic manuscripts

The Pantheon archive states editorial scope plainly: Eldarum-native canon, coherent timeline and cast across manuscripts. The Common Pantheon tome carries creation myth through Titan Wars aftermath; the Deities & Titans directory merges roster logic; Titanic Records supplies Collegium Arcanum voice on Alignment and aftermath.

Authority: When you need who did what to whom in heaven, or the Purge bracket (~6000–3000 PB in the Common chronicle), start here.

Tier 2 — Titans, planes & Underworld gazetteers

Mechanistic detail for Primes, Echoes, Shardbound, Scourge, and planar topology lives in Titans deep lore, Titan Wars, and Titans & genius loci. For transitive planes, elemental commons, and moral outer realms, use Planes of existence and Planes compendium; for Famera / entropy / gravehowl tone, Underworld gazetteer.

Tier 3 — Continental atlas & major geography

The seven continental hubs (Asceveron, Cilobria, …) describe Eldarum’s geography as the setting’s shared stage. They do not override Tier 1 theology but they do fix where “Northwake,” “Greywake,” or “Lucani” sits relative to trade and climate. Use World of Eldarum for ocean-scale framing.

Tier 4 — Integrated history synthesis

Master timeline orders mythic PB material, Titan Wars aftermath, AB civic reckoning, and Northwake politics into one narrative. It is a synthesis layer: if it ever drifted from the Common Tome, trust the Pantheon archive and fix the timeline prose—not the other way around.

The Asceveron grand chronicle sits beside it: same tier for historical claims, but it also narrates confirmed campaign beats (levels 1–14) from consolidated GM notes—useful when you need sequence without opening thirty encounter docs.

Tier 5 — Asceveron campaign (Roll20)

The campaign archive indexes handouts: Relics of the Scourge, Abrakary/Night’s Edge threads, calendar keywords (weekdays, seasons, suns/moons), and NPC/faction snapshots (Craxton, Elliswich, Underworld Collimar, Wilden, Lucani hooks). The reader-facing magic items catalog lists notable enchanted gear with links to full stats in the journal export. The Campaign journal lays out beats and motives in prose (from GM planning); use it alongside handouts for narrative continuity. This tier is authoritative for your table’s play state—session year 1559 AB, concrete item wording—while remaining subordinate to Tier 1–2 on matters like “who is Nila” or “what is the Scourge cosmologically.”

Tier 6 — Classic gazetteer articles

Towns (Towfort, Baycliff, …), realms (Stammont, Zrelia), and sidebar faith pages enrich play and preserve older prose. Treat them as expanded gazetteer: keep hooks; whenever a pantheon manuscript or atlas hub contradicts them, revise the lower-tier article toward the higher tier.

When sources disagree — rules of thumb

  1. Theology & deep time: Pantheon archive wins.
  2. Plane names / Underworld facts: Cosmology pages win over hearsay summaries.
  3. Continent shapes & placement: Atlas pages win over informal notes.
  4. “What year is it in play?” / relic stats / session NPC names: Campaign archive wins.
  5. Taste & rumor: Gazetteer flavor wins until a higher tier supersedes it.

New material should sit downstream from this hierarchy—do not invent competing creation myths beside the Common Tome without labeling them in-fiction apocrypha.
