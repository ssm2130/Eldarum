Stammont Royal Army

Regiments, ranks, and the arithmetic of endurance.

The Royal Army mixes feudal levies with professional cores—household guards sworn to aristocrats rotate through royal cognomens like seasonal dyes. Infantry trains with halberds older than taxes; artillery borrows siege theology from Storm chaplains who bless powder when clerical budgets permit and curse accountants when they do not.

Deployments

Coastal batteries anchor Zrelian-facing strategies while admiralties rehearse Tomb Bay nightmares they refuse to print in gazettes. Inland columns chase orcish coalitions near Towfort, trading hills for body counts until shamans teach terrain to bite back. Urban garrisons parade across Collimar bridges in armor bright enough to glitter dissent away—until harbor syndicates remind ministers that cobbles negotiate curfew faster than statute.

Veterans’ hospitals swell during raid seasons; physicians partner with curse-breakers when battlefield hexes outpace herbs. Spy networks speak in campaign handouts of metal disks that hunt older informants—fiction until your asset goes quiet.

Notable formations

Harbor Halberdiers rehearse amphibious drills until boarding hooks sound like bell practice—feared by corsairs who mistake discipline for ritual. Velen Dragoons ride grain roads faster than tax collectors can refigure tolls. Companies tied to the Marauders Company tradition still guard escort lines where Black Tusk feints taught quartermasters to budget for morale as a line item.
