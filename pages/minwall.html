Minwall

Northern shore town where river trade meets restless tides.

Minwall perches where the Velen spreads into salt marsh—timber pilings driven deep keep warehouses honest about their height. Fishing families paint eyes on prows to “confuse jealous waves.” The town remembers Captain Julian Welles as a local son who enlisted young and rose through the regiments until the world’s weight found him elsewhere.

Economy

Salt packing, smoked river fish, and rope braiding dominate daylight hours; smuggling dominates moonlit ones if customs blink. Festivals celebrate thaw with contests of knot speed and rude songs about admirals.

Landmarks

The Bell of Low Water rings when shoals shift; children dare each other to climb the tower during storms. A shrine to the Storm Court stands on a bluff—locals leave copper pins bent into lightning shapes.

Calendar & coin

Fish auctions quote prices in Stammont royals but record debts in tide-weeks—buyers initialed by priests swear they understood which moon (Raral, Tivoli, Honus) judged honesty that month. Smokehouses time batches to season names imported from university almanacs yet pronounced with marsh accents.
