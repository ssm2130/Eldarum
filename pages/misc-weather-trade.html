Calendar, Weather, and Trade

Practical scales harbor pilots swear by—landlubbers borrow cautiously.

Eldarum’s merchants track seasons like prayers—Zehas ash drifts eastward on peculiar winds; Mirror Reach currents shift with storms born in Titan dreams nobody invoices. Below, generalized scales useful for describing journeys aloud or in journals; they are tone dials, not meteorological dogma.

Temperature bands

Harbor logs across Stammont employ a graduated ladder from extremely cold through comfortable to extremely hot, with “very cold,” “cold,” “cool,” “warm,” “hot,” and “very hot” marching between—sailors argue where “comfortable” sits depending on rum allowances.

Wind intensity

Coastal briefings stack wind from calm through breezy, windy, very windy, to extremely windy—signal towers paint each step with different lantern grammar so pilots can argue with evidence.

Precipitation

Rain discipline stretches from dry air through sprinkling, raining, heavy rain, driving rain, freezing rain, heavy freezing rain, hail, large hail, flurries, snowing, to blizzard—each step changes which cargo tarp theology captains preach at dockside.

Civil calendars & festival math

Northwake charters commonly cite AB reckoning—“After Binding”—for contracts and coronations; temple libraries still annotate PB scales when quoting Titanic Records. Campaign journals name weekday cycles (Thorus, Harlune, Tally, Sulten, Neura) and seasonal moods (Straral, Juhna, Tizuli, Jorus) that farmers treat as theology and insurers treat as probability.

Month names shift by valley; Bromdel appears in broadsheets as a late-autumn span when mists hug the Velen—officials schedule tithes accordingly, smugglers schedule excuses. If your table tracks a specific civic year (for example 1559 AB), treat weather tables as tone controls: consistency matters less than consequence—ash that misses the vineyard still reaches the rumor mill.

Celestial gossip

Suns Vils & Vols and moons Raral, Tivoli, Honus ornament divination pamphlets sold in harbor markets; astronomers insist on ephemerides while poets insist on jealousy between the moons. Neither version keeps ships safe; both sell ink.

Trade currents

Grain descends Velen barges toward Collimar; smoked fish ascends from Minwall; obsidian shards trickle from Zehas foothills into workshops charging aesthetic premiums; rumor travels free but rarely accurate.
