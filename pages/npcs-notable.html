Notable People

Index of dossiers—heroes, villains, and everyone financially ambiguous.

Entries compile hearsay acceptable in polite company; sealed archives contain meaner truths for GMs’ eyes only. Cross-reference realms before trusting patronage chains—debts migrate across crowns faster than plague. Session-grade handouts (items, verbatim NPC facts) remain in the campaign archive; threaded plot scaffolding is on Campaign journal. Origin stories drawn from table backstories live on Player characters.

Party — Asceveron ring

These travelers move under shared patronage and intersecting vows: crowns, gods, titans, and private ledgers all lay claim to their errands. Mission threads below braid field briefings (Kul Buldar’s circle) with personal vows from each dossier—expect overlap (cures, infiltration, divinity on the march).

Amara Stormborn
Storm League courier: chart how Toros’s charge plays out through Asceveron—she arrived via Baycliff, paused in Beggars Hole (Elliswich Quag), and aims for Greenvale and the druid Gadarast.
Althinias Luumha
Vanguard-trained bounty identity—Maesis’s hunters ledger fugitives beside crown warrants. Mission boards still list Dhotarr cadres and Malvolia; privately, Malvolia’s Crushing Depths theft of his egg remains blood debt as much as briefing.
Anocheth Auvray (“Motjaba”)
Sverge Merchant Navy posting out of Narlow: map Greywake hulls, read Stammont and Zrelia cargo patterns, keep Long Trading channels honest enough to spy through.
Fiadh Stormforge
Order of the Lycan hunter—daughter of Lag’ra and Rolland, shaped by Black Tusk tragedy and mentor Darragh Stormforge; intersects Nok’tar · Lag’ra · Khartag whether she wills it or not.
Flavius Stilicho
Zrelian arcane operative—shard-bound frame, Hazoret oath, posture of artificer courier while Darkshore tallies receive uncomfortable scrutiny.
Grax Brelon
Brellon’s survivor—Dragon’s Tooth Lagoon legends, navy payback in his teeth, buried hoard rumors tying him to Greywake economics.
Inye (Teodora)
Loxodon scholar from Xaraquin—Ala, Dapany tomes, shard pilgrimage through Roafort into Towfort and the Elliswich Quag; prefers observation until civilization itself argues back.
Captain Julian Welles
Former Marauders Company commander; lone return of a Towfort decapitation strike against the Black Tusk triad; curse arc ties Sanfeld family to Kul’s patronage.
Kruyth

Mere Mayhan

Ronan MacGilroy
Hazoret theurgy—Baycliff’s Ian McCreaty threads crown chatter; deeper briefings belong to sealed compartments but surface gossip matches couriers’ tales.

Patrons & merchants

Kul Buldar bankrolls what crowns refuse to ledger: climbs toward Titan ossuaries, coin for Asnaes deliveries, favors that keep Kazon lanes open when guild wars reroute hulls. His priorities overlap the party roster—Julian's cure, Zehas reckonings—without dissolving personal vows PCs struck in smoke-filled rooms. Merchant house Asnaes flickers across contracts as patron, creditor, or defendant depending which clerk copied the ledger; frontier crews joke that Asnaes handwriting is tighter than admiralty scripture.

Villains & antagonists

Malvolia Sarka threads couture cruelty through cult economics—think poisoned philanthropy—while Black Tusk paramounts Nok'tar, Lag'ra, Khartag translate jungle law into siege poetry aimed at anyone wearing Stammont braid.

Scholars & healers

Skudana stitches theology to pathology, reading curses like marginalia—indispensable when relic sickness pretends to be mundane fever.

Soldiers & veterans

Captain Julian Welles carries Towfort's ghosts in his lungs; his dossier tracks Marauders glory, Sanfeld kin, and the curse that forces Kul's patronage into medical urgency rather than ornament.

Rumored figures

Katie O'Farrell haunts broadsheets and bard songs alike—pilgrim roads swallow names, yet at least one oath-bound hero treats every rumor as marching orders. Ian McCreaty listens more than he speaks along Baycliff's docks, threading crown updates toward Ronan when parchment would leave evidence. Seasoned sailors still mutter about Sazi, courier of an earlier era when Kul's itineraries paired sea lanes with celestial guides no chart dared publish.
