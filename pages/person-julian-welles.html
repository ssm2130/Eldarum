Captain Julian Welles

Soldier of Stammont seeking a cure even the crown’s best minds could not budget.

Enlisted with nothing but loss behind him; rose on the Zrelian frontier and southern patrol routes until captain’s braid meant hunting marauders for the crown. Towfort broke that story: a night gambit against Black Tusk leadership left him the raid’s lone return—and a curse no court physician could ledger away.

Origins

Born in Minwall on the kingdom’s northern shore. At thirteen, traveling parents bound for Greiser were slain in a goblin raid—no kin, no apprenticeship, only enlistment left. The Stammont army became his adulthood: muster rolls, Zrelian frontier service, southern coast watches around Collimar and the seaboard towns.

Military career

Assignments clustered near Darkshore against Zrelian pressure; later promotions tied him to anti-marauder work guarding commerce arteries between crown cities. Promotion to captain placed him over the Marauders Company—roughly two hundred swords meant to break ambushes on supply columns. Near Towfort, the crown set them against the Black Tusk: a week of pitched fighting bled the command white.

Julian wagered everything on decapitation. Intel named chieftain Nok’tar and shaman Lag’ra; a night strike hit the war-camp, but Khartag’s fighters slowed the wedge until leadership emerged from the tent. When Lag’ra joined the fight, the counterattack shattered the raid—Julian ordered retreat and escaped as the only soldier of the assault party to walk out. The company afterward counted thirty-two survivors of two hundred mustered for the Towfort campaign; Julian rode Collimar-ward weakened, carried at times, with a grinding pain in his chest that no surgeon, chapel, or hedge ritual could name.

The malady

Weakness followed victory like mockery—physicians baffled, chapels sympathetic yet ineffective. Skudana suggested an ancient curse requiring identification before remedy becomes plausible—miracles priced like palaces excluded all but desperate itineraries. Planning extracts thread his cure through Lucani alchemy, Celxis consultations, and shard-adjacent hazards; the beating heart of that arc is narrated on the Campaign journal, while ingredient lists and DC gates stay in the Roll20 archive.

Personal anchors

In Sanfeld he met Sylvie—a tenday courtship ended in vows and a small farmhouse bought with campaign coin. Julian’s duties kept him months in the field, but he always returned until Lily’s birth, two years later, redefined “home leave.” After discharge, visits renew a promise spoken at the door: come back whole, not merely alive.
