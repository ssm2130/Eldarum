Kruyth

Treasure diver—until the seabed offered a gem that wasn't merely treasure.

Hunting a wreck off northern rocks, Kruyth chased a glimmer into a cave where starlight seemed trapped in stone. Touch flung mind and body through void until instinct latched onto the nearest anchoring gleam—a circlet fused with a ruby pulse.

Beyond the veil

Between breaths he skimmed impossible distances: titans of vacuum scale, moons shredded as punctuation. Thought alone eventually dragged him toward what felt like home—then frostbit peaks, then captivity beneath giant canvas, ropes biting wrists while something colossal debated whether he mattered.
