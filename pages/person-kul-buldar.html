Kul Buldar

Patron of costly expeditions—volcanoes, ossuaries, rumors turned invoices.

Kul Buldar’s name surfaces wherever ambition outpaces sense: chartered digs near Titan remains, hazard pay for ash-season porters, scholarships for students brave enough to translate pre-compact tablets. Associates praise reliability of coin; detractors whisper the coin smells like vows signed in invisible ink.

Known interests

Guild briefs credit Kul with underwriting Titan strata surveys in mountainous uplands, geothermal route reconnaissance around Zehas Volcano, and maritime staging toward Kazon whenever crown licenses complicate departure. None of those threads are hobbies—they are invoices waiting for witnesses.

Field priorities

Leaked checklists attributed to Kul’s clerks read like a covenant with the world: investigate Titan remains in the uplands; secure a remedy for Captain Julian Welles’ curse—medical, divine, or otherwise invoiceable; survey hazards and ingress around Zehas; collect contracted pay from Asnaes (disputes expected—bring witnessed tallies); maintain Kazon routing knowledge with older plans that once involved a traveler called Sazi. Each ally holds private vows—Kul negotiates those separately so the party’s inner ledger stays partly opaque, which is how patrons survive politics.

Business etiquette

Contracts emphasize deliverables—relic shards, annotated maps, survivors. Payment schedules reference houses like Asnaes for supply credit; disputes travel through admiralty mediators who dress like judges and negotiate like fishmongers. When planning folders disagree with table receipts, the Campaign journal explains intent; the Roll20 archive keeps the numbers honest.
