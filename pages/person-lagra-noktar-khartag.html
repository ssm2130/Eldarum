Orcish Warchiefs of the Black Tusk

Nok’tar, Lag’ra, and Khartag — leadership triad haunting Timberwild raids.

Intelligence briefings stack these names beside sketches too flattering for honesty. Together they choreograph ambushes blending physical ferocity with spiritual insult—soldiers swear wounds ache on anniversaries like shamans nailed grief into muscle memory.

Nok’tar — Chieftain

Commands via momentum more than rhetoric—tribes rally when his tusks gleam ash-white in firelight. Prefers decisive strikes on officers over attritional siege; respects terrifyingly few diplomats because he treats treaties as weather forecasts.

Lag’ra — Shaman

Finger-pointed curses delivered in orcish vitriol became campfire warnings in royal camps—some veterans refuse to describe details, citing nausea. Rituals leverage tribal drums mimicking royal bells to sabotage signaling discipline until Towfort quartermasters doubt their own ears.

Khartag — Elite war captain

Coordinates veteran fighters in night ambushes—personal rivalry with crown officers escalated during Towfort campaigning seasons. Duel challenges scrawled in charcoal occasionally appear on supply crates—psychological warfare packaged like logistics. His cadre slowed Julian Welles’ strike long enough for Lag’ra to finish the arithmetic.
