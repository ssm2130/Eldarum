Malvolia Sarka

Noble provocateuse with smiles like drawn daggers.

Sarka walks courtly gardens in perfume sharp enough to cut treaties. House retainer accounts list “philanthropic duels” and funding for theaters where tragedies suspiciously resemble current politics. Hunters of justice place her among cult-adjacent intrigues—evidence slides through fingers like silk.

Public persona

Philanthropy galas, academy endowments, marriages suggested and withdrawn with chesslike timing. Critics vanish into social obscurity rather than graves—almost more unsettling.

Private rumors

Vanguard whisper-list claims Malvolia once stole a corrupted bronze egg from lonely peaks into Crushing Depths ritual halls—she lost two fingers to the hatchling when temple security shattered, and the child later surfaced under Maesis monks as Althinias Luumha. Whether crown judges credit dragon blood or disguised elf, the warrant file and the family ledger now share a name.

Field briefs carried into Asceveron circles name a Dortar Eterna recruitment sweep through southern Sverge cities—street sermons wrapped in civic reform language, ledgers signed with borrowed seals. Veteran justiciars treat those meetings as a respectable mask for Dhotarr cadres moving northward; the same threads surface in planning extracts summarized on the Campaign journal, where Malvolia’s late-campaign confrontation is staged as reckoning rather than rumor.

She negotiates with Dhotarr assets the way diplomats negotiate weather—never putting her own seal where ink could dry into evidence. Collectors whisper that her estate keeps curses under glass like opera masks, each labeled in a hand too elegant for witch-finders to quote in court. In Collimar, ministries learn of her favors only after budgets tilt: blackmail bouquets delivered when rose stems still carry morning dew.
