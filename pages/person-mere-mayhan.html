Mere Mayhan

Born in thunderous surf—shepherding the drowned before curiosity pulled her ashore.

Consciousness arrived with a gale: hulls splintering, sailors spinning into depths. Compulsion followed—not cruelty, but ceremony—ushering souls down tracks prepared for them, ships included, until the roster felt… attended.

Conch

A seabed conch lets her bargain with cetaceans and jaws alike: dolphins haul rope, whales shadow harbors, sharks keep honest work. Grave eyes read undeath through spray; spare-the-dying reaches farther than chapels expect. Among sea faiths some would call her border undead—she calls it tide shift.

Katie

One survivor broke pattern: Katie O'Farrell, pulled from overturned skiff because mercy flickered before doctrine. Tracking Katie ashore meant first clumsy nights in taverns, wind scraping salt skin raw—whistling air still triggers rage enough to send breakers toward noisy decks.
