Sanfeld

Southern market town remembered in soldiers’ letters home.

Sanfeld nestles among orchard hills south of the capital’s shadow—quiet enough for small farms, loud enough during harvest fairs for strangers to marry twice by accident. Veterans of supply-line campaigns speak of Sanfeld cider with reverence and regret: sweetness that tastes sharper when the next posting is Zrelian smoke on the horizon.

It was here that Julian Welles met Sylvie during a brief muster leave; their modest farmhouse became a refuge between long deployments along the Zrelian frontier. Poets in taverns cheapen the tale into ballads; veterans silence them with stares. Planning notes treat Julian’s cure as a wandering arc—Lucani alchemists, Celxis consultations, incremental hope—so Sanfeld stays emotionally loaded even when caravans bypass its gates for faster coast roads.

Harvest politics

Guild factors buy cider futures beside ribbon merchants who pretend not to weigh crown tariffs against pirate seasons. When Greywake admiralties tighten convoys, Sanfeld hedges with inland barley brokers; when plague rumors chase royal weddings, orchard lanes empty early and kitchens steam with preservative vigilance nobody prints in gazettes.
