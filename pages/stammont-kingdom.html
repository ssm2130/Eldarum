Stammont Kingdom

The Crown of Collimar and the long argument with the sea.

Stammont styles itself the “bulwark of the Northwake,” a hereditary monarchy seated at Collimar whose legitimacy rests on twin pillars: grain tithes and naval readiness. Nobles swear fealty on relic blades said to predate the Three-Crown Compact; merchants swear on shorter contracts printed on cheaper paper. Both kinds of oath bind until inconvenience becomes virtue.

Government

The monarch—currently of House Ardent—commands through a Privy Lattice of stewards who administer provinces named after rivers, saints, or famous betrayals. Tax assessors travel with soldiers; charitable hospitals travel with accountants. Cities elect guild syndics whose authority ends where royal banners begin, which is rarely where maps claim.

Military posture

The Royal Army maintains standing regiments along the Zrelian-facing coast—especially Darkshore—and rotating companies inland to chase bandits, corsairs’ landing parties, and inconvenient prophets. The Marauders Company historically policed supply arteries between urban centers after royal charters formalized what had been mercenary improvisation.

Society

Urban Stammont favors stone civic halls and loud bells; rural Stammont favors wooden shrines and louder grandmothers. Literacy is respectable among clergy and merchants; rumor travels faster than ink. Foreigners note the kingdom’s obsession with heraldic birds—hawks for vigilance, gulls for persistence, ravens for finance.

Current tensions

Spy networks watch Zrelian harbors for fleet movements while inland ministers debate succession law. Frontier temples whisper that the Storm Court’s omens multiply; scholars blame ash seasons, cynics blame budget cycles. Adventurers find employment as couriers, interrogators, and lightning rods for blame.
