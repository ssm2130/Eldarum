Towfort

Frontier fort anchoring royal roads near embattled hills.

Towfort anchors a span of royal highway threatened by intermittent orcish presses from the Timberwild. Its granaries taste perpetually of dust and tension; officers rotate through as if the fort were a hinge between civilization and laughter too harsh for polite maps.

The Black Tusk campaigns

Campaigns against the Black Tusk tribe flare when river ice retreats—warbands strike supply columns between interior cities. Night raids favor drum signals mimicking crown bells; veterans learn to sleep through noise but not through silence.

Notables among enemy leadership—Nok’tar, Lag’ra, Khartag—appear in intelligence briefings beside sketches that fail to capture their malice.

The fen road

North of the garrison, plank routes and pole-ferries sink into the Elliswich Quag—thirty-odd miles of Mire of Albermer peat where smugglers, Storm League envoys, and shard-seekers share mosquitoes with things that like buried god-wars. Most Collacht business still pretends the mud is someone else’s parish.

Roads, rivers, and the year on paper

Towfort sits on the hinge between Velen barges and march roads that pretend to be royal highways. Quartermasters plan in AB court calendars while shepherds still mutter weekday names (Thorus through Neura) learned at shrine school. When crown charters cite the Three-Crown Compact, garrison clerks duplicate filings for Collimar, for frontier judges, and for whichever mercenary company currently rents the east gate.

Supply psychology

Mid-1500s AB tensions along the Greywake mean grain here is politics with hulls attached—raids mimic drills first rehearsed at Towfort: same bell confusion, same smoke discipline, different payrolls.
