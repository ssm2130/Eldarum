Zehas Volcano

Fire cone whose ash seasons rewrite agriculture and theology.

Zehas stacks basalt shoulders against trade winds, venting steam plumes visible from coastal leagues on clear mornings. Villages on its knees mine obsidian and sulfur; villages on its throat farm brave orchards in soil fertilized by ash. Priests debate whether eruptions argue with the Storm Court or collaborate with deeper strata.

Expeditions

Patrons sponsor climbs seeking relic strata, elemental conjunctions, or miracles marketable before the next tide. Cave systems breathe hot gases that hallucinate pilgrims; maps diverge radically between expeditions because basalt remembers every quake differently.

Hazards

Lahars rewrite river geometry overnight; windborne glass storms rake anyone who treats cooling lava like scenery; worst of all, planar bleed masquerades as geothermal noise until combat rounds start ticking in initiative because the mountain now counts breath as damage.

Catastrophe season — campaign footprint

When the caldera convulses hard enough to shake distant coasts, Zehas City—ancient Zrelian bastion on the lower Resant Sea—can shift from living port to smoke column in one night. Survivors scrape shelters from driftwood uphill from creeping basalt tongues; black rock advances until lava kisses harbor foam. Temporary Grand Halls warehouse rescued relics and bell-rations while makeshift kitchens feed anyone willing to shovel ash off their bowls.

Relief rhetoric still thanks Hazoret when strangers seal planar wounds: the Obsidian Road once carried jewel-traders uphill; lava tubes afterward run hot enough that firenewts, elemental myrmidons, and worse argue over each bend. Deepest chambers reportedly host a veil-thin fracture toward the Elemental Plane of Fire—something vast enough that closing it steadies the cone, whereas failure floods tunnels until refugees sprint beneath skies raining molten ribbons. Successful intervention earns priestly gratitude and referrals toward Kazon's Overseer politics if you need coin after charity exhausts itself.
