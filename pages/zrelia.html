Zrelia

Across the Greywake: admiralties, auguries, and alloyed ambitions.

Zrelia is less a tidy nation than a shoreline coalition held together by mutual dread of neighbors and mutual appetite for distant markets. Foreigners caricature its admirals as storm-saints and its merchants as ledger-priests; natives reply that prayer and compound interest both compound if you attend services punctually.

Coalition map

Regional atlas: heartland trade web and frontier buffers.

Political structure

Power splinters among harbor magnates, temple colleges that read lightning as scripture, and a rotating Admiralty Council whose deliberations smell of tar, citrus, and ink. Treaties with Stammont oscillate between frost and formal courtesy; fishing grounds become battlefields without declarations when seasons turn hungry.

Campaign notes for travelers

Stammont soldiers along Darkshore train against Zrelian boarding tactics—nets weighted with bells, alchemical smokepots, singers hired to mimic siren calls. Intelligence networks employ harbor “listeners” who claim to distinguish prophetic dreams from drunken ones by salt content.

Culture

Zrelian festivals syncopate drums with tide clocks; masks represent faces ships left behind. Cuisine favors sharp vinegar, smoked eel, and breads dark as harbor pilings. Foreign academics study Zrelian drama because every tragedy ends with invoices.

Succession freezes & the Three-Crown mirror

When Stammont’s succession machinery jams, Admiralty Council captains treat Greywake patrols as voter intimidation hired by weather. Zrelian lawyers study Compact filings Collimar publishes—partly to exploit loopholes, partly because tariff holidays triggered by storm omens wreck predictable ledgers.

Naval planners draft year 1559 AB scenarios (see campaign archive) beside older cyclical myths: fire renewal versus inherited crown blood. Neither calendar fully persuades the other; both finance shipwrights.
